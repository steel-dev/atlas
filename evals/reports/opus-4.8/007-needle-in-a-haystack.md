# Master's in Economics at Université Paris-Saclay and the France Excellence Charpak Master's Scholarship (as of Nov 2025)

This report sets out, first, the admission requirements for the Master's in Economics (the "Master of Economics" / MoE) at Université Paris-Saclay, and second, the eligibility, benefits, and application requirements of the France Excellence Charpak Master's Scholarship, before assessing whether a student in the Paris-Saclay economics program can use the scholarship.

## 1. The Paris-Saclay Master of Economics: overview

The Master of Economics is a two-year, selective research-oriented program taught **entirely in English**, covering all fields of economics (microeconomics, macroeconomics, applied econometrics) with research projects beginning in the first year [1][2]. The first year (M1) is certified by ENS Paris-Saclay and the second year (M2) by Université Paris-Saclay, in cooperation with AgroParisTech, CentraleSupélec, and the University of Évry-Val-d'Essonne [3]. It is part of the Graduate School of Economics and Management, is taught at the Gif-sur-Yvette (Saclay Moulon) campus, and prepares students for PhD study or careers in public institutions, international organizations, consulting, and expertise [1][3]. The program is described as a demanding training requiring strong knowledge in quantitative methods, and is open to international students; in 2023–2024 the M1 cohort had 43 students [3].

## 2. Academic admission requirements (sub-question 1)

The stated target audience and entry requirements for both M1 and M2 are a bachelor's-level degree in a quantitative or economics discipline: **"BA Economics, BS Mathematics, Licence d'Economie, Licence de Mathématique"** [1][2]. The program pages state that applications are open to students with a BA in economics or a BS in mathematics [1][2].

- The program is **selective**, with limited capacity: **50 available places in M1** and **20 available places in M2** [1][2].
- For M1, the admission routes listed are Economie, Sciences sociales, and Mathématiques [1].
- **No minimum GPA, CGPA, or grade threshold is published** on the program pages [1][2].

Individual M2 courses presuppose M1-level training (e.g., intertemporal macroeconomics, welfare economics, game theory, international trade) and mathematical skills at the undergraduate (L2) level such as optimization and differential equations, which indicates the quantitative background expected of entrants [2].

## 3. Language proficiency requirements (sub-question 2)

The program is taught entirely in English [1][2][3]. The language documentation required is a **"Certificate of English level,"** which is **compulsory for non-English speakers** [1][2]. For M2, applicants may submit this certificate **or** GMAT/GRE test results instead [2]. The program pages do **not** specify a particular test (e.g., TOEFL or IELTS) or a minimum score, and no French-language requirement is stated, consistent with the program being delivered in English [1][2].

## 4. Application procedures, deadlines, and required documents (sub-question 3)

Applications are submitted through the university's online **Inception Platform** during fixed windows [1][2]:

| Stage | Application window (2026 cycle) | Indicative start of program |
|---|---|---|
| M1 Economics | 15/01/2026 to 16/03/2026 | 08/09/2026 [1] |
| M2 Economics | 25/02/2026 to 25/05/2026 | — [2] |

The ENS Paris-Saclay page also notes an external pathway for M1 via the national `monmaster.gouv.fr` portal [4].

**Required (compulsory) supporting documents:**

- *M1:* copy of diplomas; copy of passport; copy of identity document; motivation letter; all transcripts of years/semesters validated since the high-school diploma; curriculum vitae [1].
- *M2:* copy of identity document; motivation letter; a letter of recommendation or internship evaluation; a second letter of recommendation (compulsory for candidates already enrolled in higher education in France before); all transcripts since the high-school diploma; CV; and a certificate of English level (compulsory for non-English speakers) **or** GMAT/GRE results [2].

**Additional/conditional documents** include a second letter of recommendation and a letter of recommendation (both compulsory for candidates previously enrolled in higher education in France), a certificate of English level, a detailed description and hourly volume of courses taken, a VAP file (for those requesting validation of prior learning), and refugee/protection-status documents where relevant [1][2]. M2 applicants are also directed to a document listing the available local M2 choices [2].

## 5. Tracks and specializations (sub-question 4)

The first year (M1) is common across students: it is structured around **8 compulsory core courses** in micro, macro, and econometrics, plus two optional discovery courses and two research projects [1][2]. Discovery courses cover topics such as data economy, environmental economics and sustainability, experimental economics, and gender economics [3].

The second year (M2) is where specialization occurs. The program pages identify **three "fields of excellence"** [1]:

1. Trade, Geography and Development Economics
2. Public and Behavioral Economics
3. Advances and Challenges in Macroeconomics

The CEPS Paris-Saclay description frames the M2 specialization areas slightly more broadly as economic policy, applied microeconomics, theoretical economics, macroeconomics, and empirical evaluation, capped by a supervised master's thesis [3]. The M2 is described as largely flexible, allowing students to build their own curriculum under faculty supervision [1][2]. **The published entry requirements are common across the program (BA economics / BS mathematics); the sources do not indicate differing eligibility criteria by track** [1][2]. A related but distinct M2, "Économie, organisations et société (EOS)," also exists within ENS Paris-Saclay and is taught in English and open to international students [4].

## 6. Charpak Master's Scholarship: eligibility (sub-question 5)

The France Excellence Charpak Master's Scholarship is administered by the Embassy of France in India / Campus France India and is **India-specific**. To be eligible, an applicant must [5]:

- be an **Indian citizen or hold an OCI card**;
- be **30 years old or younger at the time of application**;
- be currently enrolled in or have completed studies at an Indian higher-education institution (holders of a foreign-institution degree are eligible, but preference is given to those educated at an Indian institution);
- ensure the course takes place **in France** (if a semester or more is in another country, the scholarship will not cover that period);
- be pursuing a **full-time master's degree program** (students on semester-exchange programs, internships, or research projects are not eligible);
- have **applied for admission to a French institution before applying for the scholarship**.

Knowledge of French is described as an asset but is **not mandatory** [5]. Students already pursuing a master's in France may apply for funding for the second year [5]. An applicant is **not eligible** if they are a PhD student, intend to complete only a master's thesis/training program/research project in France, or have previously been awarded the Charpak Master scholarship [5]. The scholarship covers all streams and fields of study, including economic and political sciences, at both Master 1 and Master 2 levels [5].

## 7. Charpak Master's Scholarship: benefits and obligations (sub-question 6)

Awardees obtain the status of French government scholarship holder (*Boursier du Gouvernement Français*), with the following benefits [5]:

| Benefit | Detail |
|---|---|
| Monthly living allowance | €860 per month |
| Fees | Exemption from visa fees (if applying from India) and Campus France procedure fees |
| Insurance | Student social security plus additional health insurance (*mutuelle*) |
| Accommodation | Assistance via Campus France; priority access to CROUS residences (subject to availability) |
| Meals | University-restaurant meals at €1 |
| Other | Access to social and cultural activities organized by Campus France |

Duration is **one to two years** of master's-level study [5]. The scholarship **does not cover** travel from India to France or within France, nor any other expenses related to the stay [5]. The page also notes that French government scholarship holders enrolled in national-diploma programs at French public institutions may be exempt from differentiated enrollment fees, though awardees must verify this with their institution [5].

**Obligations / grounds for revocation:** The Embassy may revoke or discontinue the scholarship if the awardee receives another French government, Erasmus, or Agence Universitaire de la Francophonie scholarship; signs an apprenticeship or professional-training contract; undertakes a non-mandatory internship; takes a gap year, academic exchange, or period abroad (in the same or a different institution); or is not granted a visa for the selected course [5].

## 8. Charpak Master's Scholarship: application requirements, documents, and deadlines (sub-question 7)

Applications are submitted on the online scholarship portal `https://scholarship.institutfrancaisindia.in`, with scanned documents [5]:

- formal passport-size photograph;
- copy of the first page of the passport (with photo and expiry date), or proof of passport application if not yet issued;
- curriculum vitae (maximum 2 pages);
- copy of the admission/acceptance letter from the French institution (email correspondence is accepted if the letter is not yet available);
- mark sheets and degrees for Class XII, Bachelor's, and Master's (including completed semesters of an ongoing degree);
- French language certificate, if any (DELF/DALF);
- employment/internship record, if applicable;
- letter(s) of recommendation (scanned signed originals, preferably on institutional letterhead with a stamp and referee contact details) [5].

Documents must be in PDF or JPEG format; incomplete or incorrectly formatted applications are rejected, and **only one application per applicant is permitted** (multiple submissions cause disqualification) — applicants should enter their top-choice university on the form [5].

**Selection process** [5]:
- *Round 1:* the selection committee evaluates academic excellence, consistency, the quality of the statement of purpose (SOP), and the overall profile. **There is no minimum CGPA or percentage required.** The SOP is plagiarism-checked, and significant AI-generated content leads to disqualification.
- *Round 2:* shortlisted candidates attend an online interview (for the 2026 cycle, between **13 and 30 April 2026**; interviews are not rescheduled).

**Deadline:** The official Campus France India page did not display an explicit application deadline at the time of review — it stated that results for the 2026 program had been finalized and candidates informed by email [5]. Third-party aggregators reporting on the 2026 cycle most commonly cite an application deadline of **30 March 2026 (23:59 IST)**, though some list 15 March 2026 and others 15 January (the January date likely refers to the separate Charpak Exchange/short-term track). The 30 March date should therefore be treated as a third-party figure rather than an officially confirmed one; historically the Charpak Master deadline falls roughly between late January and the end of March.

## 9. Can a Paris-Saclay Master of Economics student use the Charpak scholarship? (sub-question 8)

Yes — a student admitted to the Paris-Saclay Master of Economics would be eligible for the Charpak Master's Scholarship **provided they meet the India-specific criteria**. The alignment is strong on every program-related dimension:

- **Field and level:** Charpak covers all streams and fields, explicitly including economic and political sciences, at both M1 and M2 levels — which encompasses the MoE [5][1].
- **Program type:** The MoE is a full-time master's degree leading to a national diploma at a French public institution, matching Charpak's full-time requirement; such programs may even qualify for exemption from differentiated enrollment fees [5][1][3].
- **Location:** The program is delivered in France (Gif-sur-Yvette), satisfying the requirement that the course take place in France [3][5].
- **Language:** Because the MoE is taught in English and Charpak does not require French, the absence of French is not a barrier [1][5].

The binding constraints are therefore the **applicant's own profile**, not the program: the student must be an Indian citizen or OCI holder aged 30 or under, must have **applied for admission to Paris-Saclay before applying for the scholarship**, and must not be a PhD student or someone coming to France only for a thesis, training, or research project [5]. No Paris-Saclay-specific exclusion was found in the sources. One practical sequencing point follows from the calendars: Charpak requires a prior admission application (and ideally an acceptance/correspondence letter) [5], while the MoE application windows run from mid-January (M1) or late February (M2) into spring 2026 [1][2] — so applicants must coordinate the two timelines, given the Charpak interview round is in mid-to-late April 2026 [5].

## Limitations

Two genuine gaps remain in the sources. First, the Paris-Saclay program pages publish **no explicit minimum GPA and no named English-test/score requirement** beyond a generic "Certificate of English level" (or GMAT/GRE for M2) [1][2]. Second, the **official Charpak deadline for the relevant cycle is not stated** on the Campus France page (which showed results already finalized); the 30 March 2026 deadline cited here is from third-party aggregators and is not officially confirmed [5].
