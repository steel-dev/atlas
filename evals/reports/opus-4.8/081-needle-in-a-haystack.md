## SuperMoney: the consumer-lending comparison engine

SuperMoney is an online fintech that operates a financial-services *comparison platform* (and, since 2025, an AI personal-finance app), founded in 2013 by Miron Lulic and headquartered in Santa Ana, CA [2]. It is explicitly **not a direct lender** — it is an aggregator that lets a consumer submit a single application and receive competing pre-approved offers from "the world's leading banks and financial service companies," a "KAYAK-like comparison shopping experience" [5][2]. Its product verticals include credit cards, personal loans, auto loans, student loans, mortgages, business loans, banking, insurance, and stock trading [2].

SuperMoney runs a formal **affiliate/partner program** ("Monetize Solutions") offering embeddable widgets, co-branded offer-engine pages, and syndicated content across "50+ finance verticals," joinable directly (tracking.supermoney.com/signup) and also distributed through affiliate networks such as **FlexOffers**, where the Personal Loans program is "Active" and pays **up to $750 per funded loan/sale** [7][5]. This is a **CPA / pay-per-funded** model — the publisher earns when the referred user actually closes/funds, not merely on a click.

The five SuperMoney pages on Evert's Miro board — **credit cards, personal loans, auto loan refinancing, student loan refinancing, and debt consolidation loan** — map exactly, one-to-one, to the SuperMoney offerings Mesa lists in its own Affiliate Disclosure: "Personal loans ($1,000–$100,000), Auto loan refinancing, Student loan refinancing, Debt consolidation loans, Credit card comparisons… When you use SuperMoney through our referral links or secure loans/products, we may receive a commission" [1].

## Novae: the higher-touch / network-marketing partner

Novae (Novae LLC / "Novae Money") is a Black-owned fintech in Conyers, Georgia, incorporated 11/9/2014 and led by founder/President/CEO **Reco McCambry** [3][4] — **not Dorian Allen**, as the question supposed. Its full product suite covers exactly the five Novae pages on Evert's board: **debt relief** (Debt Help / debt settlement & negotiation), **trust & will plan** (Will Estate Plan + Trust Estate Plan), **life insurance**, **business debt relief** (Business Debt Help), and **business credit** — alongside credit cards, personal loans, tax prep, savings accounts, auto/student loan refinance, credit monitoring (myNovaeCredit), and a disputes manager (myNovaeDisputes) [6][3]. Mesa's disclosure independently lists Novae under both "Business Funding Partners" and "Debt Relief Services" [1].

Crucially, Novae is sold through a **direct-selling / multi-level-marketing (MLM) affiliate structure**, not a conventional publisher program. Its "Become an Affiliate" plan, presented by McCambry, has two enrollment tiers [8]:

- **Affiliate BASIC** ("Direct Sales Only"): $199 upfront + $49/mo, 40 Personal Volume, **Personal Commissions only**, includes a replicated NovaeMoney.com site and training.
- **Affiliate PRO** ("Direct Sales and Team Building"): promo $199 upfront (reg. $349) + $99/mo, 80 Personal Volume, includes myNovaeCredit and myNovaeDisputes, and earns **Personal Commissions + Team Override (downline) Commissions**, plus rank-based awards (monthly residual bonuses, Presidents Club rings, luxury-car/mortgage payments, Novae Private Stock).

The compensation document covers Affiliate Enrollment Offerings, Retail Commissions/Product Volume, Customer Acquisition Bonuses (CABs), Monthly Residual Income, and Rank Advancement — the textbook components of an MLM plan. Novae's own income disclosure states the **average participant earned $1,408 in 2023** [8].

## How the monetization actually works (analysis)

**The lead-generation funnel.** Each new page is a topical landing page built to capture search-intent traffic — a visitor Googling "debt consolidation loan" or "trust and will plan" lands on Mesa's matching page, which presents the product and routes the visitor through an affiliate/referral link or embedded SuperMoney offer-engine to the partner [7][1]. Mesa is then paid on the partner's terms: per **funded** loan/sale on SuperMoney (CPA, up to $750 per funded personal loan [5]) and per **enrollment/subscription** (plus any MLM override) on Novae [8]. In financial-services affiliate marketing generally, publishers are paid per lead (CPL), per click (CPC), or per funded/closed action (CPA/revenue share); SuperMoney here is a **CPA per-funded** model and Novae an **enrollment-subscription + override** model [5][8].

**Why two partners — the deliberate split.** SuperMoney is used for the **commoditized, high-volume, low-touch consumer-lending products** (cards and loans) where a comparison marketplace and instant competing offers convert best; Novae is used for **higher-touch, relationship-sold products** (debt relief, estate planning, life insurance, business debt relief, business credit) that require enrollment, advice, or servicing rather than a one-click comparison. Routing each topic to the partner whose model fits the product is what produces the clean five-and-five division on the board [6][5].

**Alignment with Mesa's core niche.** Mesa's stated specialties are credit repair and business funding [1]. The page set extends naturally outward from that core: consumers who arrive for credit/loan help are cross-sold the matching SuperMoney lending product, while the relationship/estate/insurance and business-credit pages cross-sell into Novae's suite — letting each landing page capture its own search intent and feed Mesa's existing client funnel [6][1].

## Contrasting the two business models (analysis)

- **SuperMoney = established, VC-backed marketplace with a standard affiliate program.** Commission economics are transparent and performance-based (up to $750 per funded loan), reputational risk is low, and the program is distributed through mainstream networks like FlexOffers [2][5]. The trade-off is that payout depends on the referred user actually *funding* — clicks alone don't pay.
- **Novae = MLM/network-marketing affiliate opportunity.** Earnings combine retail commissions with recruitment-based team overrides and residuals, but require paid enrollment ($199 upfront + $49–$99/mo) and the disclosed **average participant earned only $1,408 in 2023** [8]. Reputational risk is materially higher: although Novae holds a BBB **A+** rating and accreditation [3], BBB National Programs' **Direct Selling Self-Regulatory Council (DSSRC) Case #231-2025** (closed Nov. 4, 2025) flagged ten unsubstantiated income claims by Novae's salesforce — e.g., "Earn up to $3,000+ per referral," "Bonuses Are Paid WEEKLY and to UNLIMITED LEVELS," and "six figures per month." Novae conceded the claims did not align with FTC MLM guidance, removed six of ten, and disciplined/suspended noncompliant salespeople [4].

## Practical takeaway and due diligence

These pages show Mesa Group's monetization strategy plainly: **build SEO landing pages around financial-product search intent, then route that traffic to affiliate partners whose model matches each product** — SuperMoney for commodity lending, Novae for relationship products and business credit. For due diligence before relying on either:

- **SuperMoney** is the lower-risk affiliate — an established marketplace with a conventional, transparent CPA program; the main thing to verify is the current commission schedule for each vertical (the $750 figure is the FlexOffers Personal Loans payout and varies by category and network) [5][7].
- **Novae** warrants more caution because it is an MLM/direct-selling opportunity, not just a referral program: there are upfront and monthly costs to become an affiliate, earnings lean on recruitment/overrides, the disclosed average annual earning is modest ($1,408), and the company has a documented 2025 regulatory inquiry into exaggerated income claims by its salesforce [8][4]. Anyone evaluating Novae as an income opportunity (versus simply using it as a product to refer clients to) should weigh those costs, the FTC MLM-guidance context, and Novae's own income disclosure rather than the headline earnings claims [4].
