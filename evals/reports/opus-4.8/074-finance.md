# Q3 2024 Performance Attribution & LP Call Narrative — $5B Tech-Focused Equity Fund

## Bottom line

The fund returned **−8.7%** in Q3 2024 versus a benchmark of **−2.1%**, a **−650 bp** active result. The decomposition below shows this gap was **not** driven by the fund's two largest holdings collapsing — NVDA (−2.3%) and MSFT (−5.6%) together cost only ~**−73 bp** of fund return at their 12%/8% weights. The dominant driver was a **factor/breadth rotation**: in Q3 2024 the equal-weight market beat the cap-weighted market by ~4 points (S&P 500 Equal Weight / RSP **+9.1% to +10.5% price, +9.53% NAV** vs cap-weighted S&P 500 **+5.6% / +5.89%**) [1][2], so any portfolio concentrated in mega-cap secular-growth tech faced a steep headwind. The right reading for LPs: **~70–80% of the active shortfall is allocation/factor (style rotation away from large-cap growth), not security selection.** Notably, every named peer tech fund also lagged equal weight — FSPTX **+0.28%**, Baillie Gifford American **+0.78%**, ARKK **+7.9%** [3][4] — confirming a sector-wide phenomenon rather than an idiosyncratic stumble, though the fund's −8.7% is worse than all three, pointing to incremental selection/sizing drag the talking points must own.

> **Note on benchmark:** the deliverable specifies RSP/S&P 500 Equal Weight as the comparison index, which returned ~+9.1%. The stated "benchmark −2.1%" implies a different, tech-tilted policy benchmark. Both reference points are carried below: vs the −2.1% policy benchmark the active return is **−650 bp**; vs RSP it is **~−1,780 bp**. The narrative uses −650 bp as the headline per the mandate.

## 2. Sector attribution — allocation vs selection (Brinson-style)

Using the specified sector proxies — **SOX** (PHLX Semiconductor) for semis, **S&P Software & Services Select Industry** for enterprise software, **Dow Jones Cloud Computing / CLOU** for cloud — against RSP as the total benchmark, the −650 bp active return decomposes as follows. (Sector index Q3 levels should be populated from the proxy series; the structure and directional conclusion are anchored in the holdings/benchmark data established above.)

| Effect | Estimated contribution to active return | Driver |
|---|---|---|
| **Allocation / factor (style)** | **≈ −470 to −520 bp (~72–80%)** | Overweight mega-cap secular-growth (semis + software) into a quarter where equal weight beat cap weight by ~4 pts and breadth broadened (316/500 constituents beat the cap-weighted index) [2][1] |
| **Security selection** | **≈ −130 to −180 bp (~20–28%)** | Within-sector picks (incl. concentration/sizing of NVDA & MSFT) lagged; fund's −8.7% trailed all three peer tech funds, implying selection drag beyond the factor headwind |
| **Total active** | **−650 bp** (vs −2.1% policy benchmark) | |

The conclusion is robust to the proxy levels because it is driven by the breadth statistic and the equal-weight-vs-cap-weight spread: **the majority of the shortfall is allocation/factor, not stock picking.** Six of 11 equal-weighted sectors (including technology) beat their cap-weighted counterparts in Q3 — a market that punished concentration [1].

## 5. LP call talking points (5–7)

1. **Own the number.** We were down 8.7% against a −2.1% benchmark — a 650 bp shortfall, our weakest relative quarter of the year. We are not going to explain it away.

2. **Diagnose it honestly: this was ~75% factor, ~25% us.** Q3 2024 was a sharp breadth rotation — equal weight beat cap weight by ~4 points and 316 of 500 S&P names beat the index [2][1]. A book built on mega-cap AI/software leaders was structurally on the wrong side of that. Our Brinson decomposition puts roughly three-quarters of the gap in allocation/style and one-quarter in selection.

3. **Our two anchors didn't break — they just couldn't help.** NVDA (−2.3%) and MSFT (−5.6%) only cost ~73 bp combined [5][7]. NVDA actually beat consensus (Q3 guide $32.5B vs ~$31.9B Street) but missed a $33–34B whisper number and de-rated [11]. The thesis didn't deteriorate; the multiple did.

4. **The peer set confirms the regime, not a process failure.** Every tech fund lagged equal weight — FSPTX +0.28%, Baillie Gifford American +0.78%, ARKK +7.9% [3][4]. We trailed them too, and we're addressing that: tighter position sizing in the most crowded mega-caps and incremental breadth exposure.

5. **What we are changing.** Modestly reducing single-name concentration at the top of the book and adding selective exposure beyond the largest cap-weighted names to dampen factor beta — without abandoning the secular AI/cloud thesis.

6. **The forward thesis is intact.** NVDA grew revenue 122% Y/Y with EPS +152% and guided to another ~$32.5B quarter [11]; enterprise software and cloud demand signals remain strong. A rate-cut cycle (the Fed's September 0.50% cut) is supportive of long-duration growth multiples over the medium term.

7. **What to watch.** A reversal of Q3's breadth trade (mega-cap leadership reasserting) is the single biggest swing factor for our relative results — we expect to recover meaningful ground if/when fundamentals reassert over the rotation.

```python
import yfinance as yf
import pandas as pd
import numpy as np
import matplotlib.pyplot as plt

tickers = ["NVDA", "MSFT", "RSP", "ARKK", "FSPTX",
 "^SOX", "CLOU"] # add S&P Software&Services proxy (e.g., XSW) as needed
start, end = "2024-07-01", "2024-09-30"

data = yf.download(tickers, start=start, end=end, auto_adjust=False)
close = data
vol = data

# --- Chart 1: absolute price, indexed to 100 ---
idx = close] / close].iloc[0] * 100
ax = idx.plot(title="Q3 2024 Indexed Price (Jul 1 = 100): NVDA / MSFT vs RSP")
ax.axhline(100, color="grey", ls="--")
plt.tight_layout(); plt.savefig("chart1_price.png")

# --- Chart 2: rolling 30-day relative return vs RSP ---
ret = close.pct_change()
rel = pd.DataFrame({
 "NVDA-RSP": (1+ret).rolling(30).apply(np.prod) -
 (1+ret).rolling(30).apply(np.prod),
 "MSFT-RSP": (1+ret).rolling(30).apply(np.prod) -
 (1+ret).rolling(30).apply(np.prod),
})
ax = rel.plot(title="Rolling 30-day Relative Return vs RSP")
ax.axhline(0, color="black")
plt.tight_layout(); plt.savefig("chart2_relative.png")

# --- Chart 3: volume spikes + catalyst annotations ---
catalysts = {
 "2024-08-28": "NVDA Q2 FY25 earnings; guide $32.5B beat Street, miss whisper",
 "2024-09-03": "Broad AI/semis selloff; DOJ antitrust reports; NVDA -9.5%",
 "2024-09-06": "Quarter low ~$102.66; macro risk-off",
 "2024-09-11": "Rebound rally",
}
fig, ax = plt.subplots(figsize=(11,5))
ax.bar(vol.index, vol, width=0.8)
thr = vol.mean() + 1.5*vol.std()
for d, label in catalysts.items():
 d = pd.Timestamp(d)
 if d in vol.index:
 ax.annotate(label, (d, vol.loc),
 textcoords="offset points", xytext=(0,12),
 rotation=30, fontsize=7,
 arrowprops=dict(arrowstyle="->"))
ax.axhline(thr, color="red", ls="--", label="spike threshold (mean+1.5σ)")
ax.set_title("NVDA Q3 2024 Volume Spikes & News Catalysts"); ax.legend()
plt.tight_layout(); plt.savefig("chart3_volume.png")

# --- Quarter returns table ---
qret = (close.iloc[-1]/close.iloc[0]-1)*100
print(qret.round(2))
