## The "bubble gum" 5K at California's Great America

**Answer:** The race is the **"Bubba Gump Shrimp Co's Run Forrest Run 5K Fun Run,"** a 5K (3.1-mile) fun run/walk held at **California's Great America** in Santa Clara, California (4701 Great America Parkway) [1]. "Bubba Gump" is a homophone of "bubble gum," and this is almost certainly the race the question refers to: no race with the literal words *bubble gum* in its title was found at this park in any source.

### What the name actually was

The official event name was **Bubba Gump Shrimp Co's Run Forrest Run 5K Fun Run**, which also offered a shorter 1K [1]. It was a 5K (3.1-mile) running and walking "fun run," not another distance or format — participants describe completing it as a run or a walk, with finish ribbons for all participants [1]. The course ran through the amusement park before opening, out around the large parking lot, and then back through the park; one year drew 586 runners in total [1].

The name is a play on the *Forrest Gump* film: "Run Forrest Run" is the movie's famous line, and Bubba Gump Shrimp Co. is the Forrest Gump–themed seafood restaurant chain named after the characters Bubba and Forrest Gump. So the "bubble gum"–sounding element refers to the **Bubba Gump** brand, not to actual bubble gum, a giveaway, or a color theme [1].

### The park

The venue was **California's Great America** in Santa Clara — the park that opened in 1976 as Marriott's Great America, later became Paramount's Great America, and then Cedar Fair's California's Great America [2]. The race listing gives the address as Great America, 4701 Great America Pkwy, Santa Clara, CA 95050 [1].

### Years held

The event was a recurring annual race in the mid-to-late 2000s:

- **2005** — a contemporaneous race report dated September 18, 2005 documents running it at Great America [1]
- **2007** and **2008** — participant reviews reference running it in both years [1]

### Organizer / sponsor

The race was sponsored and branded by **Bubba Gump Shrimp Company**, the *Forrest Gump*–themed seafood restaurant chain, which had a location at the park [1]. Perks for participants included free same-day admission to Great America plus complimentary tickets to Bonfante Gardens in Gilroy [1].

### Why the park is described as "old"

The Santa Clara park is the now-closing/closed California's Great America. According to the park-history site greatamericaparks.com, **Cedar Fair sold the land at California's Great America and plans to close the park** [2]. It should not be confused with the still-operating **Six Flags Great America** in Gurnee, Illinois, which also opened in 1976 as Marriott's Great America [2]. The "Run Forrest Run" 5K ran during the park's California's Great America era.

### Distinguishing the red herring

A separate event does carry the literal title **"Bubble Gum 5K Walk/Run,"** but it is unrelated to Great America: it is presented by Oral Cancer Cause and is held at **Walsingham Park, Seminole, Florida** (12615 102nd Ave, Seminole, FL 33778), with its first annual edition on April 14, 2018 [3]. Because it is in Florida and tied to a dental-health charity, it does not match the California theme-park premise.

### Other races at California's Great America (none titled "bubble gum")

For completeness, the park hosted several other runs, none of which contain "bubble gum":

- **Carousel to Coaster** 10K Run / 5K Walk — an established annual event (its 18th edition was held under Paramount's Great America), benefiting Friends of Santa Clara Parks and Recreation
- **Mission City 5K Fun Run** — a later 5K run through the park
- **Peanuts Run & Ride** — 5K / 10K / quarter / half marathon, run 2017–2019

### Bottom line and caveat

The best-supported answer to "the 5K race at the old California Great America with 'bubble gum' in its title" is the **Bubba Gump Shrimp Co's Run Forrest Run 5K Fun Run** (~2005–2008), sponsored by Bubba Gump Shrimp Company [1]. The match rests on "Bubba Gump" being a near-homophone of "bubble gum"; no fetched source shows a race literally named with the words *bubble gum* at this park. The only literal "Bubble Gum 5K" found anywhere is the unrelated Oral Cancer Cause event in Florida [3].
