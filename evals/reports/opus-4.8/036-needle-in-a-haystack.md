## The Promotion: Red Rooster's "Quarter Chicken & Chips" Lunch Deal

**Short answer:** The chain is **Red Rooster**, and the promotion is its **Quarter Chicken & Chips lunch deal** — a quarter of roast chicken plus chips, available daily until **4pm**, priced at around **$5** circa 2015 [1].

### Identifying the chain

Every detail in the question points unambiguously to Red Rooster rather than any other Western Australian chicken chain:

- **Founded 1972 in a Perth suburb.** In 1972, the first Red Rooster store was launched by acquiring an existing chicken shop in **Kelmscott**, a suburb on Perth's south-eastern fringe [2][3].
- **Two brothers, a Greek-Australian family.** The founders were **Peter Kailis and his brother Theo**, who launched the first store along with nine other investment partners [2]. Peter was the eldest of four sons of Greek migrant George Kailis, who had run a fish shop in Perth since 1926 [2][3]. This confirms and reconciles the "two brothers" memory: the brothers were the driving force, though there were also several silent partners at the outset — Peter later bought them out to become sole owner [2][3].
- **Roast chicken and red branding.** Red Rooster deliberately distinguished itself from KFC by focusing on *roast* chicken rather than fried, and its red rooster is its core brand identity [2]. These details cleanly separate it from other WA chains.

For context on the chain's scale and trajectory: a whole roast chicken sold for $1.95 in the early days [2][3]; Peter Kailis sold the business to Coles Myer for $8.97 million in 1982, by which point it had grown to 80 stores [2][3].

### The lunchtime deal

The promotion the question describes — "portion of chicken, chips, a few bucks, cut off mid-afternoon" — is Red Rooster's long-running **Quarter Chicken & Chips lunch deal**, which the company markets simply as "your lunchtime favourite" [4]. It does not carry a flashy proprietary name like "Beat the Clock"; the name *is* essentially the description of the deal.

| Detail | Evidence |
|---|---|
| Contents | A quarter roast chicken plus a serving of chips [1] |
| Price circa 2015 | $5 [1] |
| Availability window | 11am–4pm daily [1][4] |
| Channel | In-store (later also Click & Collect) [4] |

**Confirmed active around 2015:** A Frugal Feeds listing from 2015 documents the deal as a quarter chicken and small chips for $5, available 11am–4pm daily [1]. A late-2016 OzBargain post shows a closely related $6 version — quarter chicken, small chips and a pineapple fritter — running on the same 11am–4pm daily schedule [4].

**Running since at least the 1990s:** The cheap lunchtime chicken-and-chips deal is recalled by a long-time customer as having cost "$2 on tuesdays back in 1991" (then served with a banana fritter) [4]. The same deal has continued well past 2015, appearing repeatedly at roughly $5–$5.50 through 2023–2026 with the same "until 4pm" cut-off [4].

### One distinction worth flagging

The plain lunch deal asked about should not be confused with a separate, related menu item: the **Hawaiian Pack**, developed by Peter Kailis, which paired a quarter roast chicken and chips with a pineapple and banana fritter. That item survives today as the **Tropicana / Classic Tropicana Pack**, with the banana fritter replaced by a second ring of deep-fried pineapple [2][3]. The promotion in the question is the basic quarter-chicken-and-chips lunch special, not the Tropicana.

### Bottom line

The chain is **Red Rooster** (founded 1972 in Kelmscott, Perth, by the Greek-Australian Kailis brothers, Peter and Theo) [2][3], and the promotion is its **Quarter Chicken & Chips lunch deal** — a quarter roast chicken and chips for about **$5**, available **daily until 4pm**, a deal that has run since at least the 1990s and was still active around 2015 [1][4].
