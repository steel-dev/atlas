# Relocation Analysis: Austin vs. Raleigh vs. Nashville for a Senior Mechanical Engineer

## Bottom line

For a 38-year-old mechanical engineer earning $95K with a spouse and young child, **Austin offers the strongest combination of career depth and wage, but the highest property-tax drag; Raleigh is the most balanced financial-and-quality compromise; Nashville is the weakest on job-market depth and, paradoxically, the most expensive place to access top schools.** No single city dominates on every axis. The ranking depends on how much weight you place on career optionality (favors Austin/Raleigh) versus tax simplicity (favors the no-income-tax states, Texas and Tennessee).

Note on Denver baseline: BLS excluded Colorado from its May 2024 regional wage release for data-quality reasons [1], but third-party OEWS-based data put Denver mechanical-engineer median pay at about **$128,180** (mean $126,140, ~4,530 workers) against a cost-of-living index of 112.8 [2]. At $95K you currently earn below the Denver median, and Colorado levies a 4.4% flat income tax. All three target metros have a deeper or comparable-quality engineering market relative to your current pay, and two of the three eliminate income tax entirely.

## 1. Wages and job-market depth (BLS OES, SOC 17-2141)

| Metro | ME employment | Annual mean wage | Annual median |
|---|---|---|---|
| Austin–Round Rock, TX | 1,580 | $110,700 | ~$110,700 |
| Raleigh–Cary, NC | 1,150 | $101,520 | $101,520 |
| Nashville, TN | 500 | $98,740 | ~$98,740 |

(BLS OEWS May 2023 metro estimates [3][4][5].)

Austin leads on both **wage level and absolute employment base** — roughly 3× Nashville's mechanical-engineering workforce. Current job-posting counts (LinkedIn, mid-2026) reinforce the same ranking:

| Metro | "Senior Mechanical Engineer" postings | Broader "Mechanical Engineer(ing)" postings |
|---|---|---|
| Austin | 341 | 849 |
| Raleigh | 165 | — |
| Nashville | 84 | 761 |

(LinkedIn job-search counts [source_x_linkedin].)

**Aerospace/manufacturing employer depth:**
- **Austin** — the deepest and most aerospace-weighted: SpaceX (Starlink manufacturing in Bastrop), Tesla Gigafactory, Apptronik (humanoid robotics), Firefly Aerospace, Skyways (autonomous cargo aircraft), plus Amazon data-center engineering [6].
- **Raleigh** — manufacturing and power-systems oriented: Eaton, Kiewit Power, SMT/Rise Power Systems, Jacobs (energy & power) [source_search_jobs].
- **Nashville** — manufacturing-heavy but thinner on aerospace: L3Harris (defense/aerospace), Bridgestone (tire manufacturing), and regional auto plants (Nissan in Smyrna, GM) [source_search_jobs].

On career growth, **Austin > Raleigh > Nashville** is unambiguous from both the employment base and the posting counts.

## 2. Property tax burden

| County (city) | Effective rate | Median home value (county) | Median annual bill |
|---|---|---|---|
| Travis County, TX (Austin) | **1.48%** | $523,000 | $7,727 |
| Wake County, NC (Raleigh) | ~0.51% county nominal; effective ≈0.85–0.96% with city | — | — |
| Davidson County, TN (Nashville) | **0.62%** | $386,600 | $2,390 |

(Travis [7]; Wake County FY2025–26 rate $0.5135/$100 [8]; Davidson 2026 effective rate [9].)

Austin's effective property-tax rate is roughly **2.4× Nashville's** and notably above Raleigh's. On a $500K home this is the difference between roughly $7,400/yr (Austin) and ~$3,100/yr (Nashville) — about $43,000 over ten years before appreciation-driven reassessment, though Texas caps homestead assessed-value growth at 10%/yr [7].

## 3. State income tax

| State | 2026 rate on your bracket |
|---|---|
| Texas (Austin) | 0% |
| Tennessee (Nashville) | 0% |
| North Carolina (Raleigh) | 3.99% flat (declining from 4.25% in 2025) |
| Colorado (Denver, current) | 4.4% flat |

(NC: NCDOR / G.S. 105-153.7, 3.99% for taxable years after 2025 [source_search_nc].)

On ~$95K of taxable income, North Carolina's 3.99% costs on the order of **$3,000–$3,800/yr** that Texas and Tennessee do not. This is the core tax trade-off (Section 6).

## 4. Home prices in 8+/10 school zones (2026)

| Metro | Top-rated district | Rating | Representative median home price | Affordable 8+ alternative |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Austin | Eanes ISD | 10/10 | ~$1.9M | Round Rock ISD (8/10) ~$410K–$484K; Pflugerville/Hutto corridor (e.g., Mott Elementary 9/10) |
| Raleigh | Wake County (Cary/Apex) | 8–9/10 (e.g., Green Hope Elem. 9/10) | Cary $667K, Apex $603K | Morrisville $485K; Fuquay-Varina $447K |
| Nashville | Williamson County (Franklin) | high (72% math/70% reading proficiency) | $940K–$992K median sold | Rutherford County (Murfreesboro) 8/10, ~$402K |

(Eanes/Round Rock ratings & prices [source_search_schools][10]; Cary/Apex prices [source_search_homes]; Williamson County prices [source_search_homes]; Rutherford County 8/10 and $402K median [source_search_schools].)

Key insight: **Austin offers the most attainable path to an 8/10 district** (Round Rock ISD homes near $410K, plus 9/10 elementary options in the Hutto-Pflugerville value corridor) [source_search_schools]. **Nashville's best district (Williamson County) is by far the most expensive** at ~$940K+; the affordable 8/10 option (Rutherford/Murfreesboro at ~$402K) sits further from the urban core. Raleigh's Wake County top schools are mid-priced ($485K–$667K) with several genuine 8–9/10 elementary schools.

## 5. Housing appreciation since 2020 and current trajectory

| Metro (ZHVI, March 2026) | Home value | 1-yr change | Multi-year change |
|---|---|---|---|
| Austin, TX | $422,024 | −5.9% | +2.1% |
| Nashville, TN | $452,378 | −0.7% | +12.9% |
| Raleigh, NC | $434,680 | −2.1% | +26.0% |

(Zillow March 2026 market report [10].)

Austin has given back most of its post-2020 surge and is the weakest near-term appreciation story; Raleigh shows the most durable cumulative gain. Round Rock specifically was down 5.1% YoY as of early 2026 (12 straight months of decline) [source_search_homes], and Apex −2.1% YoY [source_search_homes]. For a buyer, Austin's softness is an *entry* advantage but a weaker forward-appreciation signal.

## 6. The Texas property-tax / North Carolina income-tax trade-off

At your income (~$95K) and target home price (~$450K–$500K in an 8+ zone), the two costs roughly offset, which is why the decision is close:

- **Texas (Austin):** $0 income tax, but ~1.48% property tax. On a $475K home ≈ **$7,000/yr property tax**; income-tax savings vs. NC ≈ $3,000–$3,800/yr. Net annual tax cost relative to a no-tax baseline is dominated by the property bill.
- **North Carolina (Raleigh):** 3.99% income tax (~$3,200/yr) plus a much lower effective property tax (~$4,000–$4,500/yr on a $475K home). Combined burden is **comparable to or slightly below Austin's**, with the added benefit of stronger appreciation.
- **Tennessee (Nashville):** the lowest of both — $0 income tax *and* 0.62% property tax (~$2,900/yr on $475K) — but this advantage is undercut because reaching a top-rated district requires either a ~$940K Williamson County home or a longer commute from Rutherford County.

So Tennessee wins the pure tax math, Texas wins career depth and school *attainability*, and North Carolina is the balanced middle on tax, schools, and appreciation.

## 7. Commute to industrial zones (sub-30-minute constraint)

- **Austin:** Major employers spread across the metro — SpaceX in Bastrop (SE), Tesla in SE Austin, semiconductor/manufacturing in the north. Round Rock ISD and the Hutto-Pflugerville corridor sit in the north/northeast, within ~30 minutes of north-Austin manufacturing and Tesla/SE corridors via SH-130, but Bastrop (SpaceX) is a stretch from northern school zones. Feasible for most employers, tightest for SpaceX [6][source_search_schools].
- **Raleigh:** Research Triangle Park and the Cary/Morrisville/Apex job cluster are co-located with the best Wake County schools — the **strongest school-to-work proximity** of the three [source_search_homes][source_search_jobs].
- **Nashville:** Affordable 8/10 schools in Murfreesboro (Rutherford County) are ~35–40 minutes from central Nashville/Smyrna manufacturing, putting the sub-30-minute constraint at risk unless you target Smyrna/La Vergne employers directly [source_search_jobs].

## 8. Ten-year financial synthesis

Combining wage, taxes, housing cost, and appreciation over a 10-year horizon for a ~$475K home in an 8+ district:

| Factor | Austin | Raleigh | Nashville |
|---|---|---|---|
| Starting ME mean wage | $110,700 | $101,520 | $98,740 |
| State income tax | None | 3.99% (~$3.2K/yr) | None |
| Property tax (on ~$475K) | ~$7,000/yr (1.48%) | ~$4,300/yr | ~$2,900/yr (0.62%) |
| Affordable 8+ school home | ~$410K (Round Rock) | ~$485K–$603K | ~$402K (Murfreesboro, longer commute) |
| Recent appreciation since 2020 | +2.1% (softening) | +26.0% | +12.9% |
| Job-market depth | Strongest | Moderate | Weakest |

**Net wealth read:**
- **Austin** delivers the highest gross income (~$9K–$12K/yr more than Nashville) and easiest top-school access, but the highest property tax and the weakest recent appreciation partly offset the income advantage. Best for **career-driven wealth** (raises, job mobility, equity-heavy aerospace employers).
- **Raleigh** combines a strong wage ($101.5K), the best appreciation track record (+26%), a moderate combined tax burden, and the best school-to-work commute — the most **risk-balanced 10-year outcome**.
- **Nashville** has the lowest taxes but the lowest wage, the thinnest job market, and the worst school-affordability/commute trade-off — the lowest income ceiling despite the lowest tax floor.

## 9. Where the cheapest option underperforms

Nashville is the cleanest illustration of the tension between tax minimization and the qualitative constraints. It has zero income tax and the lowest property tax, yet:
- its mechanical-engineering employment base (500) and senior-ME postings (84) are the smallest, limiting raises and job-switching leverage over 10 years [4][source_x_linkedin];
- its top school district costs ~$940K+, and the affordable 8/10 alternative (Murfreesboro) breaks the sub-30-minute commute requirement [source_search_homes][source_search_schools].

For a career-growth-focused engineer, the salary and optionality you give up in Nashville likely exceeds the tax you save. **Austin or Raleigh better satisfy the full constraint set**, with Raleigh edging ahead on balance (commute, appreciation, moderate taxes) and Austin ahead on raw career upside and school attainability.

## Recommendation

- **Optimize for career growth and school access:** Austin (Round Rock ISD / Hutto-Pflugerville), accepting the highest property-tax bill.
- **Optimize for balanced 10-year financial outcome:** Raleigh (Wake County — Cary/Apex/Morrisville), best school-to-work commute and strongest appreciation.
- **Optimize purely for lowest taxes:** Nashville, but only if you can secure a strong employer (e.g., L3Harris, Bridgestone, Smyrna auto) and accept either a longer commute or a higher home price for top schools.
