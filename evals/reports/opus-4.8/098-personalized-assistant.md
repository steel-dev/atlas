# Relocating to Regional Queensland for Semi-Retirement: Toowoomba vs Bundaberg vs Cairns

## Bottom line

**Toowoomba is the optimal balance** of affordability, cardiac-care access, and accounting-income sustainability. It delivers the largest cost-of-living saving versus Melbourne (~43% on housing + utilities + internet, roughly **A$20,000/year**), it has on-site specialist atrial fibrillation (AF) management at St Andrew's Toowoomba Hospital — including electrophysiology, cardioversion for AF and a 24/7 cardiac catheterisation lab — and as the largest of the three centres (~140,000 people, a regional business and commercial hub) it offers the deepest part-time professional accounting market.

**Cairns is the strong fallback** if a suitable part-time role or housing emerges there first: its cardiac care is equally complete (Cairns Private Hospital runs a cardiac catheter lab, electrophysiology studies and ablation), but it is materially more expensive (only ~20% cheaper than Melbourne, ~A$9,000/year saving) because of higher rents and the highest utility bills of the three (tropical-climate cooling load).

**Bundaberg is not recommended for a managed-AF patient.** It is the cheapest on some line items, but its sole cardiac service (Advara HeartCare at Bundaberg Base Hospital) runs weekday daytime hours only (07:30–16:00) and the record does not establish on-site electrophysiology/ablation; complex AF intervention realistically requires referral to a tertiary centre well beyond 90 minutes. Its part-time professional accounting market is also the shallowest of the three.

## Cost of living

**Melbourne baseline (Numbeo, Jun 2026):** 3-bedroom rent A$3,465/month (outside centre) to A$4,712/month (city centre); basic utilities for an 85m² dwelling A$319.95/month; broadband A$79.05/month; average net salary A$6,290/month; prevailing mortgage rate 5.62% [4]. The Melbourne **median house price is ~A$983,000**.

Across the three Queensland centres, the housing+utilities+internet basket is cheaper than Melbourne in every case, but the magnitude differs sharply:

- **Toowoomba:** combined A$2,185/month vs Melbourne A$3,864/month — a **43.4% saving (~A$20,100/year)**. Utilities are the lowest of all four cities (A$108/month) [1].
- **Bundaberg:** combined A$2,247/month — a **41.9% saving (~A$19,400/year)**. Rent is marginally the cheapest (A$1,960), but utilities (A$200.69) are nearly double Toowoomba's [2].
- **Cairns:** combined A$3,111/month — only a **19.5% saving (~A$9,000/year)**. Rent (A$2,800) and utilities (A$233.33, the highest of the three, reflecting tropical-climate cooling load) erode most of the regional discount [3].

On sampled grocery items, regional prices are mixed rather than uniformly cheaper: milk 1L runs A$2.33 (Toowoomba), A$2.71 (Cairns), A$3.25 (Bundaberg); local cheese 1kg A$11.75 (Cairns), A$12.50 (Toowoomba), A$14.67 (Bundaberg) vs A$16.13 (Melbourne); chicken fillets 1kg A$13.17 (Toowoomba) but A$18.05 in Cairns vs A$13.66 in Melbourne [1][2][3][4]. Net effect: groceries are broadly comparable to or slightly below Melbourne in Toowoomba and Cairns, and not consistently cheaper in Bundaberg — so the dominant driver of the differential is housing and utilities, not groceries.

## Property price trajectory (3-bedroom homes)

All three markets rose strongly off 2020 lows, and recent medians for entry 3-bedroom houses sit at or above the A$550,000 ceiling in the question:

- **Toowoomba:** 3-bedroom house median ~A$634,000, up ~13.2% over the latest year and ~14.0% over five years.
- **Bundaberg:** 3-bedroom house median ~A$584,200, up ~15.7% over the year and ~18.0% over five years — the fastest recent growth of the three.
- **Cairns:** median house price ~A$715,000, up ~10.3% over twelve months, with rental yields above 5%, sub-1% vacancy and a median rent of A$670/week.

A 3-bedroom house **under A$550,000 is now at the bottom edge of all three markets** rather than the typical price — achievable in Bundaberg or outer/older Toowoomba stock, harder in Cairns. Buyers should expect to target older or peripheral housing to stay under the threshold.

## Specialist cardiac care within 90 minutes

This is the constraint that most cleanly separates the three for a patient with managed AF:

- **Toowoomba — passes.** St Andrew's Toowoomba Hospital runs the region's only 24/7 Heart Attack (STEMI) service with a full cardiac catheterisation lab and hybrid suite, on-call interventional cardiologists day and night, CCU/ICU, and — directly relevant to AF — **electrophysiology (EP) studies and ablation, ICD implantation, 24/7 pacemaker access, and transoesophageal echocardiogram (TOE) plus cardioversion for AF on-site** [5]. For the most complex tertiary work, The Prince Charles Hospital in Chermside (Brisbane) — Queensland's major tertiary cardiothoracic referral centre and the largest such unit in Australia — is roughly 125 km away, within a feasible drive [7]. Routine and most interventional AF needs are met locally.
- **Cairns — passes.** Cairns Private Hospital provides a cardiac medical unit, cardiac catheter laboratory and high-dependency/coronary care unit, with **electrophysiology studies (EPS), radio-frequency ablation, pacemaker and ICD insertion, TOE and Holter/loop-recorder monitoring** on-site — full EP/AF capability without leaving the city [7].
- **Bundaberg — marginal to fail for complex AF.** Advara HeartCare at Bundaberg Base Hospital is the **sole cardiac service in town**, open **07:30–16:00 weekdays only**, providing a general cardiology service (ECG, ambulatory BP and similar diagnostics) [6]. The record does not establish on-site EP/ablation; definitive AF intervention realistically means referral to a tertiary centre (Brisbane), which is well beyond 90 minutes. For an AF patient who may need procedural intervention or after-hours specialist cover, this is the weakest of the three.

## Part-time accounting market and pay

Across Queensland, part-time bookkeeping and accounting clerical work is governed by the **Clerks – Private Sector Award**, with professional accountants more commonly engaged above-award; a **A$35/hour** minimum is comfortably above the relevant award clerical minimum and is a realistic floor for an experienced corporate accountant working part-time. Market depth, not the rate, is the binding issue:

- **Toowoomba** — deepest market. As the largest of the three centres (~140,000 residents) and an established regional business, agricultural-services and commercial hub, it carries the broadest base of SME, practice and corporate finance roles likely to absorb an experienced part-timer.
- **Cairns** — moderate. A substantial city, but its economy is weighted toward tourism and hospitality, which thins the professional-services finance base relative to its population.
- **Bundaberg** — shallowest. The smallest economy of the three, with correspondingly fewer professional accounting openings.

## Retirement and financial-transition context

For someone aged 52 moving toward retirement at 60, the key thresholds: **superannuation preservation age is 60** for anyone born after 30 June 1964, so superannuation generally cannot be freely accessed until then. A **transition-to-retirement (TTR) pension** can be started from preservation age while still working — allowing a person at 60 to draw a regulated income stream (between a 4% minimum and 10% maximum of the balance annually) alongside reduced part-time earnings, smoothing the move from full-time to semi-retirement. Because the user is 52 now and plans to retire at 60, superannuation access and TTR strategies become available right at the target retirement date, not during the interim part-time years — meaning the A$35/hour part-time income must bridge the gap from relocation until age 60. (These are standard ATO settings; the retrieved sources do not contain the ATO pages, so confirm current minimum drawdown percentages and any indexation with the ATO directly before acting.)

## Trade-off ranking and recommendation

Ranking each city on the three-way balance:

| Rank | City | Affordability | Cardiac/AF access | Accounting income depth |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | **Toowoomba** | Best (~43% / ~A$20,100/yr) | On-site EP + cardioversion for AF + cath lab; Brisbane tertiary ~125 km | Deepest |
| 2 | **Cairns** | Weakest (~20% / ~A$9,000/yr) | On-site EP + RFA + cath lab | Moderate |
| 3 | **Bundaberg** | Strong (~42% / ~A$19,400/yr) | Weekday daytime clinic only; complex AF needs tertiary referral >90 min | Shallowest |

**Recommendation: Toowoomba.** It is the only location that wins, or ties for the win, on all three dimensions — top of the affordability ranking, full on-site AF/electrophysiology capability that clears the 90-minute constraint, and the deepest part-time accounting market. The managed-AF requirement effectively **eliminates Bundaberg**, whose daytime-only single cardiac service and reliance on distant tertiary referral fail the 90-minute test for procedural AF care, despite its competitive cost base. **Cairns is the fallback**: it matches Toowoomba on cardiac capability but costs roughly twice as much to live in (relative to the Melbourne saving) and has a thinner professional finance market — so it makes sense only if a confirmed part-time role at A$35/hour-plus and affordable housing materialise there before Toowoomba does. The strategy: secure the part-time accounting position first (Toowoomba preferred), confirm under-A$550,000 housing stock at the time of the move, and treat Cairns as the contingency if the Toowoomba role/housing combination does not come together within the 18-month window.
