## Bottom line

For a **2-year horizon**, the **DSP Global Innovation Overseas Equity Omni FoF** is the more suitable choice: it is broadly diversified across global innovation themes (US technology, NASDAQ-100, China internet, medical technology) and has delivered far steadier, stronger near-term returns, whereas the Edelweiss Greater China Equity Off-Shore Fund is a single-country (Greater China) bet whose performance has been highly cyclical and policy-sensitive — an unattractive risk profile to crystallise over just two years. For a **5-year horizon**, the **Edelweiss Greater China Equity Off-Shore Fund** is the better-suited vehicle: a half-decade is long enough to ride out Chinese-equity drawdowns and capture a potential mean-reversion/recovery in deeply de-rated Chinese and Taiwanese names, and its lower expense ratio (1.57% vs 1.85% regular) compounds favourably over longer holds [1][2]. Neither fund has a genuine 5-year track record reportable as of December 2024 in its current form — the DSP scheme was only launched on **14 February 2022** (≈2.9 years), so no 5-year number exists [2]; the Edelweiss feeder has a longer history but its returns are entirely a function of the underlying JPMorgan Greater China Fund.

The two funds are essentially **non-overlapping**: a Greater-China-only equity feeder versus a global-innovation multi-fund FoF. There is **no common name in their top holdings**, so the overlap is zero.

## 6. Suitability verdict

- **2-year horizon → DSP Global Innovation Overseas Equity Omni FoF.** Over a short window, diversification across geographies and a demonstrated record of steadier, stronger returns matter more than a low fee. The Edelweiss feeder's single-country China concentration (96%+ China+Taiwan), ~21–25% volatility and negative multi-year alpha make a two-year hold a binary policy/cycle bet that could easily be timed into a drawdown [1][2].
- **5-year horizon → Edelweiss Greater China Equity Off-Shore Fund.** A five-year window is long enough to absorb Chinese-equity drawdowns and capture mean-reversion in deeply de-rated China/Taiwan names; the lower 1.57% TER (vs 1.85%) compounds in the investor's favour over longer holds, and the fund has the longer, fuller track record that the DSP scheme — launched only in February 2022 — still lacks [1][2]. DSP remains a credible 5-year holding given its 10-year+ "ideal holding period" framing, but on the specific metrics compared (valuation recovery optionality, cost, and length of record) Edelweiss is the better-matched 5-year vehicle [2].
