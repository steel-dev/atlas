## Bottom line

As of June 30, 2022 Finch Therapeutics held **$104.7 million in cash and cash equivalents** (down from $133.5M at year-end 2021), and guided that this balance — together with sublease income — would fund operations **into Q1 2024** [1][2]. There is one important data correction: the ~$176.6M figure sometimes attached to Finch's mid-2022 liquidity is not supported by the filings. Two independent sources fix the actual figure at $104.7M — the Q2 2022 10-Q balance sheet and MD&A (cash and cash equivalents of $104,673K) [1] and the August 2022 EX-99.1 earnings press release ("Finch's cash and cash equivalents as of June 30, 2022 was $104.7 million") [2]. Finch's Q2 2022 balance sheet carries **no short-term-investments line**; surplus cash sat in money-market funds and interest-bearing savings accounts, so reportable liquidity for the runway analysis was $104.7M, not ~$176M [1]. The ~$176M figure instead matches the Takeda contract's "up to an additional **$176 million** in milestones" — a different number entirely [3].

On that corrected basis, Finch's implied burn rate was **roughly $5.0 million per month** ($104.7M over the ~21 months from July 2022 through end-Q1 2024), versus an actual cash-loss run-rate closer to **$8–10M/month** implied by its $47.3M six-month 2022 net loss. The Q1 2024 runway guidance was therefore credible *only* because Finch had already removed Takeda's near-term milestones from the analysis and was about to cut costs sharply — and it explicitly warned the runway could be shorter "if Takeda determines to deprioritize our collaboration" [1]. Takeda did exactly that: on **August 25, 2022** it terminated the collaboration effective **November 17, 2022**, returning all IBD rights to Finch [4]. The strategic verdict: Finch entered the Takeda review with weak leverage, a shrinking revenue stream, and a runway that depended on assumptions the review itself undermined — pushing it toward cost-cutting and, ultimately, IP monetization under time pressure rather than from a position of strength.

## Burn-rate calculation and runway reconciliation

- **Headline implied burn (per guidance):** $104.7M ÷ ~21 months (July 2022 → end Q1 2024) ≈ **$5.0M/month** [1].
- **Actual cash-loss run-rate:** the $47.3M six-month 2022 net loss implies ~$7.9M/month on a net-loss basis (~$11.4M/month operating loss in Q2 alone) [2]. Even adjusting net loss for non-cash items, the genuine cash burn ran well above the $5.0M/month the 21-month runway implies.

**Reconciliation / inconsistency flag:** The Q1 2024 guidance is only internally consistent if Finch was assuming a *step-down* in spending — which is precisely what it signaled. It had already begun cutting (the April 2022 ~20% workforce reduction; a further ~37% reduction announced September 1, 2022) and it explicitly **removed Takeda's near-term milestones** from the runway analysis [2][1]. In other words, the $104.7M-to-Q1-2024 bridge was not a steady-state extrapolation of the $8–10M/month burn but a forecast premised on aggressive cost reduction plus modest sublease income, with the company conceding it "could exhaust our available capital resources sooner than we expect" if assumptions failed or "if Takeda determines to deprioritize our collaboration" [1].

## The Takeda relationship: terms, transition, and termination

Under the **2017 worldwide collaboration agreement** for FIN-524/TAK-524 (ulcerative colitis/IBD), Finch received a **$10M upfront** payment and had collected **$4M in milestones to date**, with eligibility for **up to an additional $176M** in development/regulatory/commercial milestones plus **tiered royalties of mid- to high-single digits** on worldwide net sales [3]. An **August 2021 amendment** shifted primary development responsibility to Takeda before clinical-stage development, leaving Finch in a technical-support role through the anticipated Phase 1 trial [3].

As of Q2 2022, Takeda had informed Finch it was **reviewing the plans, timeline and budget for TAK-524** as part of a portfolio review, with no decision date given; Finch responded by stripping the related near-term milestones out of its runway math pending the outcome [2][1]. The review resolved against Finch: on **August 25, 2022** Finch announced Takeda would **terminate the collaboration effective November 17, 2022**, returning worldwide rights to FIN-524 (formerly TAK-524) and FIN-525 [4].

**Effect on financing urgency:** the collaboration was Finch's only external revenue line, and it had already collapsed — Q2 collaboration revenue of $361K was down 87% year-over-year and six-month revenue of $715K down 89% [2]. With near-term milestones removed and royalty visibility frozen during the review, Finch lost its one non-dilutive funding lever, raising the urgency of securing capital exactly when its revenue cushion was thinnest.

**Effect on negotiating leverage:** the review weakened Finch on two fronts simultaneously. Against Takeda, a partner publicly "reviewing" a program signals deprioritization, leaving Finch little leverage to extract better terms or accelerate milestones. Against capital markets, a frozen partnership plus a sub-$5M/month-implied runway and a widening net loss made any equity raise more dilutive and any asset deal more buyer-favorable — the company was negotiating from visible weakness rather than optionality.

## IP monetization strategy and the strategic-risk timeline

Finch's principal residual value sat in its **microbiome patent estate**. Following the January 2023 discontinuation of the **CP101 Phase 3 (PRISM4) trial** in recurrent *C. difficile*, the company explicitly pivoted to **"realizing the value of its IP estate"** through licensing and litigation [1]. Its enforcement program later produced a **$25 million jury verdict (August 9, 2024)** against Ferring Pharmaceuticals/Rebiotix, finding Rebyota infringed three fecal-transplant patents — **US Pat. Nos. 10,675,309, 11,541,080, and 10,251,914** (the last licensed from the University of Minnesota) — plus future royalties, and it pursued infringement claims touching the Seres/Nestlé Vowst franchise [1].

The **strategic risk** the runway created is direct: a short, cost-cut-dependent runway with no partnership revenue forced IP monetization to happen **under time pressure**. Litigation value (the Ferring verdict) and any licensing/settlement realize over multi-year horizons that a ~21-month runway cannot wait out, so a cash-constrained seller must accept discounted licensing terms, settlement haircuts, or an outright asset sale rather than holding for full value. That tension played out to its conclusion: Finch reduced its workforce **from 150+ employees to one** and filed **Chapter 11 in Delaware**, seeking a **§363 sale** of the microbiome patent portfolio and contingent litigation proceeds [1] — the most value-eroding form of monetization, distressed-sale, rather than the negotiated licensing it had hoped to pursue from strength.

## Trade-offs the timeline forced

- **Cost reduction vs. lost pipeline value:** the 2022 restructurings (~20% in April, ~37% in September) and the eventual CP101 pause genuinely lowered R&D burn and extended runway — Finch's cash glided from $104.7M (6/30/22) to $85.3M (9/30/22) to $71.0M (12/31/22), with later guidance of operations "into 2025" [1]. But the same cuts hollowed out the clinical pipeline (CP101, FIN-211, the returned FIN-524/FIN-525), converting Finch from a clinical-stage developer into an IP-holding shell. Runway was bought with future upside.
- **Operational flexibility vs. dilutive financing / asset sales:** during the Takeda review window Finch faced a classic squeeze — raise dilutive equity into a depressed, partnership-uncertain story, or sell/license assets at a discount. With the term loan ($15M at 8.43%) and heavy lease obligations limiting net liquidity, and with the partnership lever frozen, it had little room to preserve flexibility; the choices it could make were all value-dilutive.

**Going-concern and listing context:** Finch's Q2 2022 10-Q framed funding adequacy as a risk rather than issuing a formal substantial-doubt "going concern" statement; explicit "at least the next 12 months / dependent on raising additional capital" language first appeared in the **Q3 2022 10-Q** (cash $85.3M as of 9/30/22) [1]. Listing-compliance trouble came later still: Finch traded as **Nasdaq: FNCH** and received a **minimum-bid-price deficiency letter on November 15, 2023** (35 consecutive business days below $1.00) — a late-2023 development, not a mid-2022 one [1]. This sequencing reinforces the core finding: in mid-2022 the company looked solvent on paper, but the Takeda review had already removed the lever that made its Q1-2024 runway, and its negotiating posture, sustainable — and from there the path ran through deepening losses, a pipeline wind-down, and distressed IP monetization.
