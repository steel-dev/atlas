# FutureMeds — Company Profile & Private-Equity Assessment

*Prepared from publicly available information up to 17 November 2025. Financial figures are drawn primarily from the audited consolidated accounts of FUTUREMEDS LTD (UK company no. 12521988) and from the UK Companies House register; commercial and transaction detail from company, investor and trade-press disclosures. Several third-party aggregators (Tracxn, PitchBook) carry inconsistent or placeholder figures, which are flagged where relevant.*

## 1. Business identity and ownership

FutureMeds is an independent **clinical-trial site network / Site Management Organisation (SMO)** serving global pharmaceutical sponsors and CROs. It owns and operates dedicated research sites and provides end-to-end trial services — patient recruitment, trial execution and data delivery — alongside hybrid/decentralised (DCT) capabilities including home nursing visits ("FutureMeds @home") [1][2][3]. It positions itself as Europe's leading independent site network specialising in decentralised clinical trials, and following its 2023 acquisition of emovis describes itself as the first DCT site network in Europe [2][3].

- **Sector:** Clinical research / outsourced pharma services (SIC 74909, professional/scientific/technical activities) [4].
- **Founded:** Late 2019; UK parent FUTUREMEDS LTD incorporated 17 March 2020 [1][4].
- **Founders / leadership:** Dr Radoslaw Janiak (CEO), Marcin Gondek (CFO), Paul Chambers (Chairman) [1][4].
- **HQ:** Liverpool, England (group parent); core operations in Poland and across Europe [5][3].
- **Ownership:** Backed by **HGL Private Capital from 2021**, which pursued a buy-and-build strategy and exited in 2025 [3]. In **July 2025, Phoenix Equity Partners** (a UK PE firm focused on founder-entrepreneurs) acquired control via a bid vehicle ("Futuremeds Bidco Limited" / "Renifer Bidco Limited"), registered as a person with significant control holding 75–100% of shares and votes from 23 July 2025 [6][4]. Phoenix's own framing emphasises partnership with the founder team, who retain operating leadership [7][8].

**Group structure (all 100%-owned subsidiaries):** FutureMeds sp. z o.o. (Poland); two Ukrainian site entities (Evidence Based Medicine & GCP Research Institute; Medical Center "Pulse"); two Bulgarian entities (Medical Center FutureMeds EOOD; "Ascendent"); emovis GmbH (Germany); FutureMeds Spain S.L. (Spain); and Community Pharmacology Services Limited (Scotland) [4].

## 5. Peer and valuation-multiple comparison

FutureMeds sits in the clinical-research-site (CRS) / SMO segment, a fragmented market in the early stages of consolidation [9].

- **Transaction multiples (Capital IQ/PitchBook to June 2025):** pharma-services M&A median **19.8x EBITDA**, average 21.6x, top quartile >27x; public CRO comparables at median **17.8x EBITDA / 1.8x revenue** [9].
- **Site-specific multiples:** mid-market site operators transact **6x–12x EBITDA**; standalone sites <$5M EBITDA at 4x–6x; mid-sized $5–10M EBITDA at 7x–9x; larger differentiated networks ($10M+ EBITDA) into the low-teens [9].
- **Roll-up arbitrage:** platforms aggregated at single-digit EBITDA multiples are frequently exited at mid-teens, capturing scale-driven multiple expansion [9].
- **Margin benchmark:** EBITDA margins of **25–50%** are cited as the attractive/normal range for CRS businesses [9]. FutureMeds' ~12.4% operating margin (EBITDA somewhat higher) screens **below** that benchmark, suggesting room for margin improvement but also reflecting its labour-heavy, multi-country, rapidly scaling and DCT-inclusive model.

On growth, FutureMeds (+73% revenue YoY; ~97% three-year CAGR) compares favourably with peers such as Velocity Clinical Research, which publicly frames its ambition around scaling toward $500M revenue [9]. On scale and margin, however, FutureMeds is still a mid-market operator: at ~£27m (~$34m) revenue and an estimated single-digit-£m EBITDA, it sits in the 6x–12x site multiple band rather than the premium $10M+ EBITDA / low-teens tier.

## 6. Private-equity investment thesis

**Why it is attractive.** FutureMeds offers a rare combination in European clinical research: a profitable, fast-growing (≈73% YoY), founder-led, pan-European owned-site platform with DCT capability, strong sponsor/CRO endorsements and a clean audit and regulatory track record [1][4]. It operates in a structurally growing, fragmenting market that strategics and PE are actively consolidating [9].

**Value-creation levers:**
1. **Buy-and-build / roll-up** — continue acquiring sites and small networks at single-digit EBITDA multiples and integrate into the platform for multiple arbitrage on exit [9][3]. FutureMeds has already executed this (emovis, CPS Research) [2][4].
2. **Geographic expansion** — extend the owned-site footprint (recent additions in Romania, Ukraine and London) and deepen presence in Germany, Spain and the UK via health-system partnerships [1][3].
3. **Margin improvement** — lift operating/EBITDA margin from ~12% toward sector norms via site-utilisation gains, post-deal integration synergies, shared services and pricing on differentiated DCT/oncology offerings [4][9].
4. **Mix shift to higher-value therapeutics** — scale the ambulatory oncology programme and other premium indications that command valuation premiums [1][9].
5. **Backlog and sponsor diversification** — convert preferred-network status (IQVIA/Medpace/Fortrea) into contracted backlog that supports both revenue visibility and exit multiple [1][9].

**Likely exit routes & timeline:** Given Phoenix's 2025 entry, a typical 4–6 year hold points to an exit around 2029–2031. Most probable routes, in order: (i) **strategic sale** to a CRO or larger site platform (strategics drove ~90% of site M&A in 2021–Q2 2025) [9]; (ii) **secondary buyout** to a larger PE sponsor building a platform; (iii) **IPO**, the least likely given size. The HGL→Phoenix transition is itself a demonstrated secondary/sponsor-to-sponsor exit precedent [3][8].

**Valuation considerations:** As a sub-$10M-EBITDA mid-market operator, FutureMeds plausibly trades in the 6x–12x EBITDA range today, with a credible path to the low-teens (and pharma-services comps near ~20x) if it scales EBITDA above $10M, lifts margins and diversifies backlog [9]. The undisclosed Phoenix entry price limits precision, but the value-creation case rests on **EBITDA growth × multiple expansion**, partly funded by leverage already evident in the ~77% gearing and the November 2025 secured facilities [4][6].

## 7. Top 5 risks

1. **Margin / execution risk.** Operating margin (~12.4%) is well below the 25–50% benchmark for sites; the buy-and-build thesis depends on integration and margin uplift that may not materialise, while wages already consume ~50% of revenue [4][9].
2. **Leverage / financing risk.** ~77% gearing, interest cover down to 2.1x in FY2024, and new secured charges layered on at the 2025 buyout raise sensitivity to rate moves and earnings shortfalls [4][6].
3. **Customer / sponsor concentration risk.** Revenue depends on pharma sponsors and CROs and on preferred-network relationships; no client-concentration data is disclosed, so a single sponsor or CRO program loss could be material and is unquantifiable from public sources [1][9].
4. **Geographic / geopolitical risk.** Material operations in **Ukraine** (two site entities) carry war-related disruption risk; broader exposure to Polish, Bulgarian and multi-currency operations adds FX and regulatory complexity [4].
5. **Regulatory & market risk.** Performance hinges on continued biopharma R&D spend and on maintaining FDA/EMA/national-authority compliance across multiple jurisdictions; audit failures, protocol delays or a downturn in trial volumes would hit enrolment-driven revenue, and rising sector competition could compress acquisition and exit multiples [1][9].
