# vTv Therapeutics: Equity-Financing Strategy and Shareholder-Dilution Risk

## Bottom line

vTv Therapeutics (Nasdaq: VTVT) is a clinical/late-stage, pre-revenue biotech that funds itself almost entirely with equity, and its capital structure is now dominated by a large, low-strike warrant overhang. Share-based compensation (SBC) is rising in step with a workforce expansion around its lead program, but it is a comparatively small dilution lever next to the equity raises themselves. The dominant dilution driver is the financing structure: a September 2025 $80.0 million private placement (PIPE) and a February 2024 private placement that together left **13,714,356 warrants outstanding at September 30, 2025** [1] — more than triple the company's **3,938,018 Class A shares then outstanding** [1]. Of those warrants, **8,470,624 are pre-funded warrants exercisable at $0.01 with no expiration**, i.e. shares in all but name; the **5,243,732 Common Warrants are struck at $22.71**, far above the recent trading range, so they act as a longer-dated overhang [1][2]. Quarter-over-quarter SBC is escalating (+18.1% Q3 2024→Q3 2025), but the surge is dwarfed by the warrant-driven dilution. Net: vTv's strategy is to repeatedly recapitalize through insider/strategic equity, accepting heavy, structurally embedded dilution as the price of funding a single high-cost Phase 3 program, while using equity comp to conserve cash for that program.

## Share-based compensation trends

**Full year 2023 → 2024.** Total share-based compensation expense rose from **$1,578K in FY2023** (R&D $425K, G&A $1,153K) to **$2,757K in FY2024** (R&D $742K, G&A $2,015K) — an increase of **$1,179K, or +74.7%** [3]. For context, FY2022 SBC was $1,272K [3], so the bulk of the multi-year step-up occurred in 2024, ahead of the further escalation seen in 2025.

**Q3 2024 → Q3 2025.** Combining the two line items from the Q3 2025 10-Q: R&D stock-based compensation rose from **$228K to $311K (+$83K, +36.4%)** and G&A from **$625K to $696K (+$71K, +11.4%)**, for a **total quarterly SBC increase of $154K, from $853K to $1,007K (+18.1%)** [1]. On a nine-month basis the same pattern is larger in absolute terms: R&D $511K→$845K, G&A $1,421K→$1,905K, total **$1,932K→$2,750K (+$818K, +42.3%)** [1].

The SBC escalation tracks a broader cost ramp around the lead program rather than a pay-driven spike: Q3 2025 R&D expense jumped to $7.0M from $3.2M (+117.7%), driven by cadisegliatin clinical-trial and manufacturing spend plus a Novo Nordisk license-milestone accrual [1].

## Stock options granted and what the strike price signals

During the nine months ended September 30, 2025, vTv granted **388,816 options at a weighted-average exercise price of $19.67**, with a weighted-average grant-date fair value of $15.00 (vs $14.85 in 9M2024) [1]. Options vest ratably over a four-year service period (three years for the options issued in connection with the February 27, 2024 private placement) and carry a ten-year term [1]. After grants, forfeitures (66,384 at $112.16) and exercises (20,719 at $11.81), options outstanding were **1,007,306 at a weighted-average $25.07**, with only 372,211 exercisable (at a much higher $37.11) [1].

Two retention signals stand out. First, the company is actively re-pricing incentives down the cap table: the $19.67 average strike on 2025 grants is well below the $35.85 weighted-average strike on the options carried into 2025 and the $112.16 strike on the expired/forfeited block, indicating fresh, lower-strike grants meant to re-incentivize staff after the stock's decline (and the 1-for-40 reverse split of November 2023) [1]. Second, the aggregate intrinsic value of in-the-money awards at September 30, 2025 was **de minimis** — i.e. the option pool was essentially underwater [1] — which is why the company leans on continued grants and a $6.2M unrecognized-SBC pipeline (to be recognized over ~2.6 years) to keep compensation meaningful without spending cash [1]. For a pre-revenue issuer burning cash on a Phase 3 trial, this is a classic cash-conservation/retention trade: pay talent in equity to preserve runway, at the cost of incremental dilution.

## Warrant overhang and dilution mechanics

The warrant book is the heart of the dilution story. Activity in the nine months to September 30, 2025: 3,970,587 warrants outstanding at the start of the year (at $0.01), **9,805,446 granted at a weighted-average $12.15**, 61,677 exercised, leaving **13,714,356 outstanding (all exercisable) at a weighted-average $8.69** [1]. The composition matters far more than the blended strike:

- **Pre-funded warrants: 8,470,624 shares, $0.01 strike, no expiration** [1]. These are economically equivalent to issued shares — they are already included in vTv's weighted-average basic and diluted share count — and will convert with near-certainty. The Feb 2024 and Sept 2025 PIPEs used a 4.99%/9.99% beneficial-ownership cap (adjustable to 19.99%) that paces, but does not prevent, conversion [1].
- **Common warrants: 5,243,732 shares, $22.71 strike** (the S-3 lists 5,271,405 common-warrant shares at a weighted-average $22.98) [1][4][2]. With the strike far above the unit price ($15.265) and recent trading levels, these are out-of-the-money and unlikely to be exercised near term — a deferred overhang that only converts (and only then dilutes/raises cash) if the stock roughly doubles, e.g. on positive CATT1 data.

Against **3,938,018 Class A shares outstanding**, the warrant overhang alone is ~3.5x the share base; layering in the 1,007,306 options and 241 remaining Class B shares, the company discloses **6,308,952 potentially dilutive securities excluded from EPS at September 30, 2025** (options 1,007,306 + common warrants 5,301,405 + Class B 241), up sharply from 1,344,537 a year earlier [1]. (Pre-funded warrants are not in that 6.3M figure because they already sit in the share count.) Diluted EPS equals basic for all periods because losses make all of these antidilutive for now — but they are real future share issuance [1].

## Equity financing transactions, 2023–2025

vTv has funded operations "primarily through… private placements of common and preferred equity," and as of September 30, 2025 was "evaluating several financing strategies to increase our cash reserves" [1]. Recent transactions:

- **September 2025 $80.0M PIPE** (securities purchase agreement Aug 29, 2025; closed ~Sept 3, 2025): 5,243,732 Units, comprising 682,018 Class A shares (at $15.265), pre-funded warrants for 4,561,714 shares ($15.255, $0.01 strike) and Common Warrants for 5,243,732 shares ($22.71 strike) [1][2]. Investors were existing holders **Samsara BioCapital and the T1D Fund (Breakthrough T1D Venture, LLC)** plus new investors **Trails Edge Capital Partners and Invus**; TD Cowen and Evercore ISI were lead placement agents [2]. (The G42 Healthcare/Aculeus Capital entities named in the research prompt do not appear in vTv's disclosures for this period; the strategic backers here are Samsara, the T1D Fund, Trails Edge and Invus [2].)
- **February 2024 private placement** (closed Feb 27, 2024): 464,377 Class A shares at $11.81 plus pre-funded warrants for 3,853,997 shares ($11.80; $0.01 strike), for **aggregate gross proceeds of approximately $51.0 million before offering expenses** [3][5]; on March 5, 2024, 116,493 shares were exchanged for 116,590 pre-funded warrants [1]. The securities purchase agreement also granted investors the right to purchase up to an additional $30.0 million of Class A common stock on or before August 27, 2025 [3].
- **TD Cowen ATM offering:** vTv sold 179,400 Class A shares for net proceeds of $2.5M in 9M2024, and **no shares under the ATM in Q3/9M 2025** [1]; usage is constrained while public float stays below $75M [1].

Financing inflows totaled **$77.8M in 9M2025** (driven by the PIPE) versus $52.6M in 9M2024 [1]. The reliance on equity is reinforced by the November 20, 2023 1-for-40 reverse stock split, executed to restore per-share price/listing compliance after prior dilution [1].

## Liquidity, runway and pipeline context

The capital appetite is set by a single, expensive program. **Cadisegliatin (TTP399)** is a liver-selective glucokinase activator in the **CATT1 Phase 3 trial** as an adjunctive oral therapy to insulin for **type 1 diabetes**, with topline data expected in **2H 2026** [2]. Funding that trial drove R&D to $7.0M in Q3 2025 (+117.7% y/y) [1]. Liquidity at September 30, 2025: **$98.5M cash** (up from $36.7M at year-start, almost entirely the PIPE), against a **$319.6M accumulated deficit** and a 9M2025 operating cash burn of $16.0M [1]. Management expressly frames the PIPE as providing runway "following topline data" in 2H 2026 [2] — implying the cash is sized to reach that readout but likely not to commercialization, so further equity raises are foreseeable.

## Synthesis: dilution-management approach and impact on existing shareholders

The three trends compound. (1) Escalating SBC (+18.1% q/q, +42.3% over nine months) plus 388,816 new low-strike options [1] continuously add to the option pool, even though their near-term dilution is modest and their intrinsic value is currently nil [1]. (2) The pre-funded warrants are effectively pre-committed share issuance (8.47M shares at $0.01, no expiry) that will convert regardless of stock performance [1]. (3) The 5.24M common warrants at $22.71 are a contingent second wave that converts only on a sharp re-rating [1][2] — paradoxically, good clinical news in 2H 2026 would trigger that conversion and a further dilution/cash-raise event.

For existing common holders the cumulative arithmetic is stark: ~3.94M shares outstanding sit beneath ~8.47M near-certain pre-funded-warrant shares plus ~6.3M other dilutive securities (options + common warrants) [1]. vTv's "dilution management" is largely structural rather than mitigating: it routes new money through insider/strategic investors (Samsara, T1D Fund, MacAndrews — which still controls ~37.8% of voting power [1]) and uses pre-funded warrants and beneficial-ownership caps to manage ownership-percentage optics and timing, not to limit the absolute number of shares ultimately issued. SBC is deployed to conserve cash for the Phase 3 program, accepting persistent equity dilution as the funding model. The result: a company adequately capitalized to its 2H 2026 data readout, but one whose existing shareholders should expect continued, embedded dilution — immediate from pre-funded-warrant conversion and option vesting, and a further large tranche if cadisegliatin succeeds and the $22.71 common warrants come into the money.
