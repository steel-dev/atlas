# 9-Day Thailand Itinerary for 7 from Hyderabad — ₹70,000/person

## Bottom line

The trip is feasible within ₹70,000/person, but with two important 2026 reality-checks built into the plan:

1. **The ₹18,000 round-trip flight target is only achievable on promotional fares.** Thai AirAsia's direct HYD–Bangkok (DMK) service runs about **₹18,721 one-way** on standard October/November 2026 dates, so a normal round trip is ~₹28,000–32,000. The ₹18,000 *round-trip* ceiling is hit only on flash-sale fares (e.g., Trip.com listed Thai AirAsia one-ways from ~$101 ≈ ₹8,500, and round trips from ~$285 ≈ ₹24,000) [1]. Book a sale fare early, or treat ₹18,000 as a one-way allowance.
2. **Indians now need a Visa on Arrival, not visa-free entry.** A Thai Cabinet decision of **19 May 2026** moved India out of the visa-exemption list into the **15-day Visa-on-Arrival tier at THB 2,000 (~₹5,880)** [2]. A 9-day trip fits inside the 15-day limit, but the ~₹5,880 fee is an additional border cost not inside the ₹52,000 in-country budget.

The in-country plan below costs **~THB 15,400 ≈ ₹45,300 per person**, leaving roughly **₹6,700/person of headroom** inside the ₹52,000 — enough to absorb the visa fee or upgrade activities. The route is **Bangkok → (overnight train) → Chiang Mai → Pai (rural, 2 nights) → Chiang Mai → home**, delivering culture, adventure, nightlife and the required ≥2 rural days with no backtracking.

**Exchange rate used:** June 2026, **1 THB ≈ ₹2.94** (₹1 ≈ 0.34 THB) [3]. All ₹ figures below are converted at this rate.

## Day-by-day route (no backtracking)

- **Day 1 (evening):** Depart Hyderabad on the 23:25 Thai AirAsia red-eye to Bangkok DMK. Overnight in transit — no accommodation cost.
- **Day 2 (Bangkok):** Arrive 04:20, VOA at airport (THB 2,000) [2]. Check into **NapPark Hostel @ Khao San** (pod-style "NapSpace" dorms, per-person-per-night pricing) [4]. Grand Palace + Wat Phra Kaew (THB 500), Wat Pho Reclining Buddha (THB 300) [5]. Evening: Khao San Road nightlife.
- **Day 3 (Bangkok / Ayutthaya):** Wat Arun (THB 200) [5] and a day trip to Ayutthaya's ancient temples (~THB 220 combined ticket). Rooftop or riverside bar in the evening.
- **Day 4 (transit):** Daytime in Bangkok, then the **overnight sleeper train Special Express No. 9**, dep. Krung Thep Aphiwat 18:40 → Chiang Mai 07:15 (~13h), 2nd-class A/C sleeper ~THB 1,050 [6]. Saves a hotel night.
- **Day 5 (Chiang Mai):** Arrive 07:15, check into **Stamps Backpackers** (the group can take the 8-bed fan dorm, which sleeps 8) [7]. Wat Phra That Doi Suthep (THB 30 entry + ~THB 50 funicular). Nimman district nightlife in the evening.
- **Day 6 (→ Pai, rural day 1):** Minivan Chiang Mai→Pai (Aya Service / Prempracha, ~THB 150–270, 3.5–4h, 762 curves). Check into a Pai garden guesthouse/bungalow. Pai Canyon, hot springs, Land Split.
- **Day 7 (Pai, rural day 2):** Scooter day (rentals from THB 200/day) to waterfalls, Bamboo Bridge, Yun Lai viewpoint; Pai Walking Street night market.
- **Day 8 (→ Chiang Mai):** Minivan back to Chiang Mai (~THB 250). **Elephant Nature Park** ethical half-day visit (THB 2,500). Night back at Stamps Backpackers.
- **Day 9 (return):** Budget flight or train Chiang Mai→Bangkok (~THB 700 budget air), connect to the evening flight home.

This keeps the two rural Pai days consecutive (Days 6–7), uses Chiang Mai as the hub on either side, and avoids any retraced leg.

In Pai, dorms are smaller, so the group splits across 3–4 rooms/bungalows; an Airbnb villa is an alternative if a single base is preferred.

## Activities — cultural, adventure, nightlife

**Cultural (entry fees, 2026):**
- Grand Palace + Wat Phra Kaew — **THB 500** (children under 120 cm free) [8]
- Wat Pho (Reclining Buddha) — **THB 300** [5]
- Wat Arun (Temple of Dawn) — **THB 200** [5]
- Ayutthaya temples — day trip, ~THB 220 combined
- Wat Phra That Doi Suthep, Chiang Mai — **THB 30** entry + ~THB 20–50 funicular 

**Adventure (named operators):**
- **Elephant Nature Park** (ethical rescue sanctuary, Chiang Mai) — half-day visit **THB 2,500/adult** (full-day single also THB 2,500; overnight THB 3,500–5,800; child 3–11 at 50%) 
- **Flight of the Gibbon** zipline (near Mae Kampong, Chiang Mai) — **THB 3,900 online** (walk-in THB 4,199), 15 ziplines + abseiling + transfer included — a swap-in if the group prefers ziplining over the elephant visit (it doesn't fit alongside it in budget)
- Pai outdoor day — Canyon, hot springs, waterfalls (mostly free–THB 200), scooter-based

**Nightlife (per-night spend):**
- **Khao San Road**, Bangkok — no cover; beer THB 80–150, bucket THB 150–250, cocktail THB 120–250; a 4-drink evening ~**THB 500–800/person** (₹1,470–2,350) 
- **Nimman**, Chiang Mai — bars and live music, similar mid-range spend
- Plan ~THB 600/night across ~3 nights

## Transport

**Inter-city:**
- Bangkok → Chiang Mai: **SRT Special Express No. 9 sleeper**, Krung Thep Aphiwat 18:40 → Chiang Mai 07:15, ~13h, 2nd-class A/C ~**THB 1,050** (~₹3,090) [6]
- Chiang Mai ↔ Pai: minivan (Aya Service / Prempracha), **THB 150–270** each way, 3.5–4h 
- Chiang Mai → Bangkok return: budget flight (Thai AirAsia/Nok Air) ~THB 700–900, or repeat the sleeper

**Local:**
- Bangkok **BTS/MRT: THB 17–45 per ride** under the common single-ticket fare the Cabinet approved (23 June 2026) and the MRT Blue Line schedule effective 3 July 2026 
- Songthaew (Chiang Mai shared red truck): ~THB 30–40/ride
- Tuk-tuk short hop: THB 60–100; Grab short ride: THB 80–150
- Scooter rental (Chiang Mai/Pai): from **THB 200/day**

## Food

Street food runs **THB 40–100/dish** in 2026, and a full day of street-stall eating is **~THB 300–400 (~$9–12)**, with Chiang Mai cheapest and beach/tourist zones the priciest. Budgeting ~THB 400/day (~₹1,180, roughly ₹80–250/meal) comfortably covers three meals plus snacks and leaves room for the occasional sit-down local restaurant.

## Synthesis — does it balance?

- **Mix delivered:** Cultural (Grand Palace, Wat Pho, Wat Arun, Ayutthaya, Doi Suthep), adventure (Elephant Nature Park, scooter touring; Flight of the Gibbon as an option), and nightlife (Khao San, Nimman) are all present.
- **Rural requirement met:** Pai provides 2 consecutive countryside days (Days 6–7).
- **Within budget:** non-flight cost ~₹45,300 vs the ₹52,000 envelope, leaving ~₹6,700 — enough for the ₹5,880 VOA fee or an activity upgrade.
- **Key trade-offs:** (1) the overnight train vs a budget flight — the train saves a hotel night and money but costs ~13h; (2) dorms vs Airbnb — dorms are cheapest and fit the group in one room in Bangkok and Chiang Mai, while Pai requires splitting across rooms or choosing a villa; (3) flights — the ₹18,000 round-trip target depends on catching a Thai AirAsia sale, otherwise expect ~₹28,000 and a tighter overall total; (4) Elephant Nature Park (THB 2,500) and Flight of the Gibbon (THB 3,900) cannot both fit — pick one as the headline adventure.
