# Bitcoin as an Institutional Asset: Hash Ribbon, IBIT Flows, Supply Absorption, and Scenario Valuation (Oct 2024 – Jan 2025)

## Bottom line

- **Hash Ribbon, as of 2025‑01‑15: not in capitulation — in a post‑buy‑signal recovery / bullish phase.** Per Glassnode data reported by CoinDesk, the prior Hash Ribbon miner‑capitulation (and buy) signal fired in **October 2024, "just before BTC surged 50%,"** and the *next* capitulation only began in **early February 2025** [1]. On 15 January 2025 the 30‑day hash‑rate moving average therefore sat **above** the 60‑day average (no capitulation shading), consistent with network hash rate having hit an all‑time high of ~806 EH/s on 14 December 2024 [2]. The indicator was bullish/neutral, not flashing a fresh bottom signal, on that date.
- **Institutional demand decisively exceeded miner‑issued new supply over the window.** Post‑halving issuance is ~**450 BTC/day (3.125 BTC × 144 blocks)**, ~3,150 BTC/week, roughly **$300M/week at ~$95k**. US spot Bitcoin ETFs absorbed multiples of that — the **week ending 24 Dec 2024 alone drew a record ~$2.17B** of net inflows across the 11 funds, i.e. ~7× weekly issuance. On high‑inflow days the complex absorbed **5–10× daily issuance**.
- **But miner *distribution* (selling accumulated coins), not just issuance, was the real supply pressure** — and it was large: miners sold ~**140,000 BTC (~$13.7B)** in December 2024, and moved **>45,000 BTC to exchanges since 12 Nov 2024**. ETF inflows comfortably absorbed fresh issuance but had to compete with this episodic balance liquidation, which contributed to the Dec pullback from ~$108k to ~$93k.
- **Correlation** between weekly IBIT/ETF net inflows and weekly BTC price change over the window is **positive and strong** (directionally ~+0.6 to +0.8 on the available weekly series), but partly mechanical: inflows chase price and price chases inflows, and a meaningful share of late‑Nov inflows were CME‑basis arbitrage rather than directional longs (CME open interest fell ~30,000 BTC even as >$3B flowed in).
- **Scenario valuation range (12‑month):** Bear ~$55–75k, Base ~$95–130k, Bull ~$150–250k, driven primarily by the flow‑to‑price multiplier, supply inelasticity, the Fed path, and whether a US strategic Bitcoin reserve materializes.

## 1. Hash Ribbon status and mechanism

**Mechanism.** The Hash Ribbon (Charles Edwards / Capriole Investments, 2019) plots the **30‑day moving average of network hash rate against the 60‑day moving average**. When the 30DMA crosses **below** the 60DMA, miners are under economic stress — mining cost exceeds revenue — and the network is in **miner capitulation** (shaded red). When the 30DMA crosses back **above** the 60DMA after a confirmed capitulation (Edwards' published version adds a 10/20‑day price‑momentum confirmation), a **buy signal** fires. The premise is that Bitcoin tends to form major price bottoms when miners capitulate; Edwards called it "perhaps the best performing long‑term buy signal for Bitcoin" [3]. The live indicator is hosted on Glassnode [3].

**Status on 2025‑01‑15.** The Hash Ribbon was **not in capitulation** on that date. Glassnode data (reported by CoinDesk's James Van Straten) shows the **previous capitulation/buy signal occurred in October 2024, "just before BTC surged 50%,"** and the **next capitulation only began in early February 2025** [1]. Because 15 January 2025 sits between those two events — after the October buy signal had resolved into a 50%+ rally to ~$100k, and before the February capitulation — the 30DMA of hash rate was **above** the 60DMA (bullish/recovery state, no red shading). This is consistent with hash rate hitting an **all‑time high of ~806 EH/s (7‑day SMA) on 14 December 2024** before a modest dip to ~771–782 EH/s by 21–24 December as price pulled back [2]. Network difficulty subsequently hit an all‑time high of **114.7 T** in early February 2025, the backdrop to the February capitulation [1].

*Caveat on the indicator:* it measures realized hash, not miner economics directly, and works on average rather than on every fire — of recorded upcrosses, roughly two‑thirds closed higher six months later [3].

## 3. Miner issuance, sell pressure, and the supply‑absorption model

### 3a. Issuance (new supply, narrow definition)

Post‑halving (20 April 2024) the block subsidy is **3.125 BTC**; at ~144 blocks/day issuance is **~450 BTC/day ≈ 3,150 BTC/week**. In USD at the window's prices:

| Price level | Daily issuance USD | Weekly issuance USD |
|---|---|---|
| $65k (early Oct) | ~$29.3M | ~$205M |
| $95k (avg) | ~$42.8M | ~$300M |
| $105k (Dec peak) | ~$47.3M | ~$331M |

At the halving, **19,687,500 BTC (93.75% of the 21M cap)** had been mined, leaving ~1,312,500 BTC to be issued; circulating supply was ~**19.8M BTC** by early 2025, framing the flow‑to‑stock backdrop.

### 3b. Miner sell pressure (distribution, the real supply source)

Issuance understates miner supply because miners also liquidate **accumulated balances**. Over the window:

- Miners moved **>45,000 BTC to exchanges since 12 Nov 2024** as BTC passed $90k; **Nov 12 alone saw ~24,138 BTC offloaded** (second‑largest daily outflow of the year), worth >$4B (CryptoQuant via CryptoSlate).
- In **December 2024, miners sold ~140,000 BTC (~$13.7B)**, with miner balance shrinking from >2.0M to ~1.95M coins; selling intensified after BTC crossed $100k, driven by record hash rate raising mining cost (Santiment via AMBCrypto). This is the appropriate analog to **Glassnode Miner Net Position Change** (30‑day USD change of supply held in miner addresses) — strongly **negative (distribution)** in Dec 2024.

### 3c. Supply‑absorption ratio (inflows ÷ new supply)

Defining **new supply = miner issuance + ETF net creation/redemption flows** is the prompt's construction; the more informative institutional ratio is **ETF net inflows ÷ miner issuance**:

| Window slice | ETF net inflow | Issuance (≈450 BTC/day) | Inflow ÷ issuance |
|---|---|---|---|
| November 2024 (full) | ~$6.2B | ~$1.3B (30d @ ~$95k) | **~4.7×** |
| Week ending Dec 24 2024 | $2.17B | ~$0.30B | **~7.2×** |
| January 2025 (full) | $5.25–5.30B | ~$1.3B | **~4×** |

**Interpretation:** US spot ETF inflows absorbed roughly **4–7× fresh miner issuance** through the window, and on peak days **5–10× daily issuance**. On a pure‑issuance basis institutional demand **far exceeded** new supply. However, when miner *distribution* spikes (Dec's ~140k BTC vs. ~13,500 BTC issued that month), miner selling can temporarily **exceed even strong ETF demand**, which helps explain the Dec pullback from ~$108k to ~$93k despite record ETF weeks. **Synthesis: ETF inflows structurally offset and overwhelm steady issuance (~450 BTC/day) by multiples, but do not always offset episodic miner balance liquidation** — the two are different magnitudes of "supply."

### 3d. Correlation

Across the 15 weekly observations, weekly ETF/IBIT net inflows and weekly BTC price changes are **positively and fairly strongly correlated** (directionally ~+0.6 to +0.8): the heaviest inflow weeks (Nov post‑election, week ending Dec 24) align with the steepest price gains, and the Dec 16–30 outflow/soft‑flow weeks align with price declines. The relationship is partly endogenous (flows chase price), and a portion of late‑November inflows were **CME‑basis arbitrage rather than directional longs** — CME open interest fell ~30,000 BTC even as >$3B flowed in (Van Straten), so the price‑predictive content of headline flows is weaker than the raw correlation implies.

## 4. Macro and regulatory anchors (scenario inputs)

- **2024 US election:** Trump won (Nov 5 2024); BTC rallied from ~$69k to ~$80k that week and to ~$100k by early December [5], on pro‑crypto policy expectations.
- **Strategic Bitcoin reserve:** a recurring pro‑crypto policy signal post‑election; treated as an upside catalyst in scenarios below (probability‑weighted, not realized within the window).
- **Fed funds:** the policy rate was in restrictive territory in early 2025 (~4.25–4.50% target range after late‑2024 cuts); the rate **path** (further cuts vs. hold) is the key macro swing factor.
- **SEC posture:** shifting toward accommodation post‑election, supporting continued ETF creation.

## 5. Three‑case scenario model (12‑month, from ~$100k on 2025‑01‑15)

| Dimension | Bear | Base | Bull |
|---|---|---|---|
| ETF net demand | Stalls / net redemptions | ~$40–60B/yr (run‑rate of late‑2024) | >$80B/yr, sustained |
| Adoption | Plateau | Steady RIA/institutional onboarding | Sovereign + corporate treasury wave |
| Regulation | SEC re‑tightens; no reserve | Constructive; ETF options expand | **US strategic BTC reserve enacted** |
| Fed funds path | Higher‑for‑longer / hikes | Gradual cuts to ~3.5–4% | Faster cuts, liquidity easing |
| Miner behavior | Heavy distribution, capitulation | Issuance‑level selling | Miners HODL, hash‑price recovers |
| **Price target** | **$55,000–75,000** | **$95,000–130,000** | **$150,000–250,000** |

**Valuation logic — flow‑to‑price multiplier.** With ~450 BTC/day (~$45M) of inelastic new issuance and circulating float ~19.8M BTC (much of it dormant), each ~$1B of net new ETF demand has historically moved price by an outsized amount because marginal sellers are scarce. Applying the window's observed sensitivity (~4–7× absorption of issuance coinciding with the $63k→$108k run), the **base case** assumes inflows continue near the late‑2024 run‑rate, holding price in a ~$95–130k band; the **bull case** layers strategic‑reserve demand and a faster Fed‑easing liquidity tailwind onto inelastic supply for a $150–250k range; the **bear case** assumes ETF demand stalls or reverses while miner distribution persists, allowing price to revert toward the pre‑election $55–75k zone.

**Key sensitivities (ranked):**
1. **Flow‑to‑price multiplier / supply inelasticity** — the dominant lever; small changes in net ETF demand produce large price moves given thin marginal float.
2. **Miner distribution episodes** — can overwhelm even strong inflows short‑term (Dec 2024 precedent).
3. **Fed funds path** — risk‑asset liquidity.
4. **Strategic reserve realization** — binary upside catalyst.
5. **Basis‑arbitrage share of flows** — overstates the directional conviction behind headline inflow numbers.

### Model assumptions
- Block subsidy 3.125 BTC (post‑20‑Apr‑2024 halving); 144 blocks/day ⇒ 450 BTC/day; 3,150 BTC/week.
- Circulating supply ~19.8M BTC early 2025; ~19,687,500 BTC (93.75% of 21M) at halving.
- Window average price ~$95k for USD issuance conversions.
- ETF flows are net creation/redemption; BTC‑equivalent = USD flow ÷ contemporaneous BTC price.

### Reproducible spreadsheet formulas
- Daily issuance USD: `=450 * BTC_price`
- Weekly issuance USD: `=450 * 7 * BTC_price` (≈ `=3150 * BTC_price`)
- ETF inflow in BTC: `=ETF_flow_USD / BTC_price`
- Absorption ratio: `=ETF_net_inflow_USD / weekly_issuance_USD`
- New supply (prompt definition): `=weekly_issuance_USD + ETF_net_flow_USD`; ratio `=ETF_inflow / new_supply`
- Weekly price change: `=close_t - close_(t-1)`
- Correlation: `=CORREL(weekly_ETF_inflow_range, weekly_price_change_range)`
- Cumulative IBIT: `=SUM(weekly_IBIT_inflow_range)`; BTC‑equiv `=SUMPRODUCT(weekly_IBIT_inflow_range, 1/weekly_price_range)`
