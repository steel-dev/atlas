## Why this route is the best fit for the budget

The best-value route is **Bangkok → Chiang Mai → Pai → Chiang Mai → Bangkok**, not southern islands. Northern Thailand is the cheapest region in the cost sources, with backpacker days around **$20–25/day**, while general Thailand backpacker costs run **฿700–1,200/day**, including dorms **฿180–420**, food **฿180–350**, local transport **฿70–180**, and low-cost activities **฿0–180** [5]. Bangkok is still feasible at **฿900–1,200/day** for a backpacker day using dorms, street food, BTS/MRT, and walking [6]. Pai adds the required rural/countryside time while staying cheap at **฿600–1,200/day**, with hostel/bungalow beds **฿200–400**, street meals **฿60–120**, and many free/cheap sights [7].

For long-distance travel, use the **overnight Bangkok–Chiang Mai train** where possible: it takes **11–13 hours**, second-class AC sleeper berths are commonly **฿600–800** within a wider **฿600–1,500** sleeper range, and it replaces one hostel night [8]. The bus is cheaper at **฿400–700** and takes **9–11 hours**, but the sleeper train is better for seven people because it is safer, more comfortable, and avoids arriving exhausted [8]. Chiang Mai–Pai is a straightforward **3–4 hour minivan** route; terminal tickets can be about **฿200 each way**, while online/agent fares run **฿250–750** [9][7].

## Day-by-day itinerary and cost breakdown

### Day 1 — Hyderabad → Bangkok: Khao San/Rambutri arrival night

**Sleep:** Bed Station Khaosan or Lub d Bangkok Silom. 
**Plan:** Land at Bangkok DMK/BKK, check into the hostel, get local cash/SIM, walk Rambutri/Khao San Road, and keep the first night flexible for street food and low-cost bars. Bangkok street-food dishes are typically **฿40–100**, with many dishes averaging **฿40–80**, and Khao San/Silom dorms are commonly **฿200–350**, with social hostels like NapPark/Lub d around **฿250–400** [6]. 
**Transport:** Airport bus/rail + BTS/MRT/Grab split; BTS/MRT rides cost **฿16–62**, taxis start at **฿35**, and Grab is usually **20–30%** above metered taxis [6].

### Day 2 — Bangkok temples + Chao Phraya river + overnight to Chiang Mai

**Sleep:** Overnight train or bus to Chiang Mai. 
**Plan:** Grand Palace/Wat Phra Kaew in the morning, Wat Pho and Wat Arun by river boat, then street-food dinner before the evening sleeper train. The Grand Palace foreigner ticket is **฿500**, Wat Pho is **฿300**, and Wat Arun foreigner adult entry is **฿200** [18][19][20]. Chao Phraya Express boats cost **฿15–32**, while the tourist boat is **฿60** [6]. 
**Transport:** Evening Bangkok–Chiang Mai train, ideally second-class AC sleeper. Standard sleeper estimate **฿800**, within the source range of **฿600–1,500** [8].

### Day 4 — Doi Suthep + ethical elephant half-day

**Sleep:** Chiang Mai. 
**Plan:** Early Doi Suthep, then Kanta Elephant Sanctuary half-day. Doi Suthep entry is **฿30**, optional tram **฿50**, and red songthaew access from the Old City is about **฿50–80 each way** or Grab return **฿200–350** [21]. Kanta Elephant Sanctuary half-day is **฿1,500/adult** and includes hotel pickup/drop-off, sanctuary entry, clothing, feeding/medicine prep, Mae Taeng River bathing, fruit/tea break, and return transfer; it does not include a hot lunch, so budget **฿150–300** for lunch [22]. 
**Nightlife:** Nimman or Tha Phae bars, capped to protect the budget.

### Day 5 — Chiang Mai → Pai: countryside arrival + Pai Canyon sunset

**Sleep:** Mad Monkey Pai 8-bed private dorm, Ark Pai, or a group Airbnb near Mae Hi/Pai countryside. 
**Plan:** Morning minivan Chiang Mai→Pai, check in, explore the river/countryside lanes, then Pai Canyon sunset and Pai Walking Street. Chiang Mai–Pai takes **3–4 hours**; direct terminal tickets can be about **฿200**, while agent/online fares can be **฿250–750** [9][7]. Pai Canyon has **฿0** entry, and Pai Walking Street runs roughly **17:00–23:00** with no entry fee [7][24]. 
**Transport in Pai:** For seven people, use a negotiated songthaew/private pickup rather than scooters unless riders are licensed and experienced; Travelfish gives private day hire at **฿1,500–2,000** and return trips to Pai Canyon/hot springs around **฿400/person including waiting** [9].

Add Yun Lai Viewpoint **฿50**, Ban Santichon **฿50** plus tea **฿60–100**, and Pai countryside stops. 
**Cheaper/closer option:** Pai Canyon **฿0**, Memorial Bridge **฿0**, Pam Bok/Mor Paeng waterfalls **฿0–20**, Land Split donation stop, Boon Ko Ku So bamboo bridge **฿30**, Yun Lai **฿50**, and Tha Pai Hot Springs only if the group accepts the **฿300 foreigner entry** [7].

## Local transport plan for the group

- **Bangkok:** Use BTS/MRT and river boats first; BTS/MRT rides are **฿16–62**, Chao Phraya Express boats **฿15–32**, taxis start at **฿35**, and tuk-tuks at **฿100–200** are for one short experience only, not routine transport [6].
- **Bangkok ↔ Chiang Mai:** Prefer second-class AC sleeper train at **฿600–800** if available; bus at **฿400–700** is the fallback [8].
- **Chiang Mai city:** Use red songthaews at **฿30–40/person** in town; Doi Suthep shared red songthaew is **฿50–80 each way**, with Grab return about **฿200–350** [21].
- **Chiang Mai ↔ Pai:** Book terminal minivans for about **฿200–250 each way** where possible; agent/online prices can be **฿350–500** or more [7][9].
- **Pai countryside:** For seven travelers, a private songthaew/pickup is more practical than seven scooters; private day hire is **฿1,500–2,000 total**, while individual return trips to Pai Canyon/hot springs can be **฿400/person including waiting** [9]. Scooters are cheaper at **฿150–200/day + ฿80–120 fuel**, but only licensed, experienced riders should use them on rural roads [7].

## Booking rules to keep it under ₹52,000 in-country

1. **Lock transport early:** the plan assumes two long overnight legs at about **฿800/person** each; if sleeper trains rise toward the **฿1,500** upper range, switch one leg to the **฿400–700** bus or use the ₹3,465 buffer [8].
2. **Book 8-bed dorms directly:** seven people should book a whole 8-bed dorm where possible in Bangkok, Chiang Mai and Pai; this minimizes privacy problems without paying hotel prices [10][12][14].
3. **Cap nightlife:** the plan includes nightlife but not club bottle service or rooftop cocktails; Bangkok can remain budget-friendly only if the group uses street food and BTS/MRT [6].
4. **Choose one Chiang Mai splurge:** Kanta Elephant Sanctuary at **฿1,500** is included; if flights or accommodation run high, swap it for Mama Noi cooking class from **฿1,000** and recover **~฿500/person** [22][23].
5. **Keep Pai flexible:** Pai’s free/cheap sights make the budget work. If the group wants Tha Pai Hot Springs at **฿300** and Tham Lod Cave on the same day, use a shared vehicle and avoid multiple separate songthaew fares [7][25][9].

**Final verdict:** The itinerary is logistically feasible on consecutive days and fits the requested **₹52,000/person in-country ceiling** with a planned spend of **₹48,536/person**, provided the group books dorm-style accommodation, uses overnight Bangkok–Chiang Mai transport, keeps Pai as the countryside segment, and secures international flights close to the separate **₹18,000/person** target.
