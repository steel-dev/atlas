# Admission requirements for the Master’s in Economics at Université Paris-Saclay and France Excellence Charpak Master’s Scholarship requirements

## Scope and source status

This report reflects the official Université Paris-Saclay and Campus France India pages retrieved during the research session. Where the current page had moved beyond the 2025 scholarship cycle, I treat the official current Campus France India page as controlling for eligibility, benefits, documents and selection rules, and use preserved 2025/2026 deadline evidence only to describe the **typical timing** of the Charpak Master’s cycle. The question asks about students pursuing the Master’s in Economics at Université Paris-Saclay; the Charpak Master’s scholarship is not program-specific, but it applies to full-time Master’s study in France across fields, so the Paris-Saclay Economics Master fits the field/level criterion if the applicant also satisfies Charpak’s nationality, age, admission and other rules [1].

## 1. Université Paris-Saclay Master’s in Economics: admission requirements and eligibility

### Program identity and selectivity

Université Paris-Saclay presents the Economics Master as a **full-time academic programme**, taught in **English** [2]. The M1 page describes the Master of Economics as a **two-year selective programme** taught in English, covering microeconomics, macroeconomics and applied econometrics, with research projects starting in the first year [2]. The university’s general Master’s admissions page also warns that admission to Paris-Saclay Master’s programmes is selective because places are limited [3].

The programme is therefore best understood as a selective, English-taught research-oriented economics track rather than an open-enrolment Master’s. The official stated capacities reinforce this:

| Entry point | Places / capacity | Source |
|---|---:|---|
| M1 Economics | 50 | [2] |
| M2 Economics | 20 | [4] |

### Academic eligibility / required background

For M1 Economics, the programme page states that applications are open to students with a **BA in economics** or a **BS in mathematics** [2]. In the entry-requirements section, the page lists the target background as: **BA Economics, BS Mathematics, Licence d’Économie, Licence de Mathématique** [2].

For M2 Economics, the official page uses the same target-audience wording — **BA Economics, BS Mathematics, Licence d’Économie, Licence de Mathématique** — while Paris-Saclay’s general international application guidance separately says that applicants to the second year of a Master’s, i.e. M2, are candidates who have validated an M1 or **240 ECTS** [4][5]. Practically, this means a direct M2 applicant should not read the M2 page as accepting only a bachelor-level qualification; direct entry to M2 normally presupposes sufficient prior Master’s-level study or equivalent credits, in addition to the economics/mathematics fit [5].

CEPS, the Paris-Saclay economics centre associated with the programme, gives a slightly broader but consistent formulation: applicants should hold a bachelor’s degree in economics or mathematics; other degrees in sciences or social sciences from strong universities may be considered [6]. That broader statement should be treated as supplementary rather than overriding the official programme page.

### Language expectations

The programme is taught in English [2][4]. The application-document lists require evidence of English ability in some cases: for M1, an English-level certificate is listed among additional documents and is compulsory for non-English speakers; GMAT or GRE may also be used where indicated [2]. For M2, an English-level certificate is also specified as compulsory for non-English speakers, with GMAT or GRE mentioned as an alternative/related supporting document [4].

### Application platform and procedure

Paris-Saclay’s application route depends on level and applicant status. For M1, international applicants must follow the university’s international-student instructions, and the platform can be INCEPTION or MonMaster depending on the programme list and applicant situation [5]. For applicants living in a country covered by the **Études en France** procedure, Paris-Saclay’s table says they first apply through the relevant platform, then, if admitted, go to Études en France under the “I am accepted” section [5]. For M2, Paris-Saclay states that applications must be submitted on the **INCEPTION** platform [3].

The university also emphasizes that an admission certificate or authorisation to enrol is conditional: administrative formalities and the regulatory qualifications required for entry are checked at registration, and admitted students must accept admission on the designated platform [3]. Foreign nationals who have not been enrolled in French higher education during the previous two academic years must provide the contact details of two academic referees [5].

### Application periods shown on the programme pages

The current programme pages displayed the following application windows:

| Entry point | Application period shown | Platform/source |
|---|---|---|
| M1 Economics | 15 January 2026 – 16 March 2026 | INCEPTION / programme page [2] |
| M2 Economics | 25 February 2026 – 25 May 2026 | INCEPTION / programme page [4] |

Because the question is framed “as of Nov 2025,” the safest interpretation is that these were the relevant upcoming windows visible on the pages after the 2025 cycle. Paris-Saclay also states generally that opening and closing dates depend on the course, appear on course pages under “Application Procedures,” and applications cannot be submitted outside those dates [3].

## 2. France Excellence Charpak Master’s Scholarship: eligibility for a Paris-Saclay Economics student

### Applicability to the programme

The France Excellence Charpak Master’s Scholarship is designed for **one to two years of Master’s-level study in France** and is open to **all streams and fields of study** [1]. It therefore can apply to the Université Paris-Saclay Master’s in Economics, provided the student is applying to or admitted to that full-time Master’s programme and meets the scholarship’s personal eligibility rules [1]. The official 2025 results document also shows Université Paris-Saclay among French host institutions for Charpak Master awardees or waitlisted candidates, confirming that Paris-Saclay is within the universe of eligible French institutions [7].

### Who is eligible

A student pursuing the Paris-Saclay Economics Master would need to satisfy all of the following Charpak Master conditions:

| Eligibility rule | Requirement | Source |
|---|---|---|
| Nationality / status | Must be an Indian citizen or hold an OCI card | [1] |
| Age | Must be 30 years old or younger at the time of application | [1] |
| Prior education link to India | Must be currently enrolled in or have completed studies at an Indian higher-education institution; foreign-degree holders are eligible, but preference is given to those educated in India | [1] |
| Admission step | Must have applied for admission to a French institution before applying for the scholarship | [1] |
| Programme format | Must be a full-time Master’s degree programme | [1] |
| Location | Course must take place in France; if one semester or more is in another country, that period is not covered | [1] |
| French language | French knowledge is an asset, not mandatory | [1] |
| Current Master’s students in France | Students already pursuing a Master’s in France can apply for second-year funding | [1] |

### Who is not eligible

The official Campus France India page excludes applicants who are applying for a semester exchange, internship or research project rather than a full-time Master’s degree; PhD students; students intending only to complete a Master’s thesis, training programme or research project in France; and previous Charpak Master scholarship awardees [1]. It also gives cancellation/discontinuation grounds after award, including holding another French government, Erasmus or Agence Universitaire de la Francophonie scholarship, signing an apprenticeship or professional training contract, taking a gap year or academic exchange/duration abroad, or not receiving a visa for the selected course [1].

For the Paris-Saclay Economics Master specifically, the key risk points are: (1) the student must be an Indian citizen/OCI and 30 or younger; (2) the Economics Master must be pursued as full-time degree study in France; and (3) scholarship funding may not cover any substantial period abroad if the study plan includes one semester or more outside France [1].

## 3. Charpak Master’s Scholarship: benefits and limits relevant to budgeting

The scholarship is partial support, not a full cost-of-attendance award. Awardees receive French government scholarship-holder status and the following benefits:

| Benefit | Amount / description | Source |
|---|---|---|
| Monthly living allowance | €860 per month | [1] |
| Visa and Campus France fees | Exemption from visa fee if applying from India, and exemption from Campus France procedure fees | [1] |
| Health cover | Student social security and additional health insurance / mutuelle | [1] |
| Accommodation assistance | Campus France help finding affordable student accommodation; priority access to CROUS residences subject to availability | [1] |
| Meals | Access to €1 meals at university restaurants | [1] |
| Activities | Access to social and cultural activities organised by Campus France | [1] |

The official page states that the scholarship does **not** cover travel expenses from India to France or within France, nor other expenses related to the awardee’s stay in France [1]. It also notes that French government scholarship holders enrolled in programmes leading to a national diploma at French public higher-education institutions may be exempt from differentiated enrolment fees, but the awardee must check the institution’s policy [1]. For a Paris-Saclay Economics applicant, this means tuition-fee treatment should be verified directly with Paris-Saclay after admission rather than assumed from the scholarship alone.

## 4. Charpak Master’s Scholarship: application requirements and required documents

Applications must be submitted through the online scholarship portal identified by Campus France India: **https://scholarship.institutfrancaisindia.in** [1]. The official page requires scanned copies of the following:

| Required item | Details | Source |
|---|---|---|
| Photograph | Formal passport-size photograph | [1] |
| Passport | First page of passport showing photo and expiry date; if passport will be obtained after the deadline, attach proof of passport application | [1] |
| CV | Maximum 2 pages | [1] |
| French admission / acceptance letter | Copy of admission or acceptance letter from the French higher-education institution | [1] |
| Academic records | Mark sheets and degrees for Higher Secondary School (XII), Bachelor’s and Master’s degrees, including completed semesters of an ongoing degree | [1] |
| French-language certificate | DELF/DALF if any; not mandatory if none | [1] |
| Employment / internship record | If applicable | [1] |
| Recommendation letters | From current Indian university/employer or last attended institution; scanned typed and signed originals; preferably scholarship-specific, with referee signature/contact details and, if possible, institutional letterhead and stamp | [1] |

If the Paris-Saclay admission/acceptance letter has not arrived before the scholarship deadline, Campus France India allows the applicant to attach email correspondence showing that the admission process is underway or that the letter will arrive after the scholarship deadline [1]. If applying to multiple French universities, the applicant must submit only one scholarship application and use the top-choice programme in the form; multiple online applications lead to disqualification [1].

The document-format rule is strict: documents must be in PDF or JPEG format; Word, RTF, ODT, TXT, MSG files, ZIP folders, screenshots and social-media scans are not accepted, and applications with missing or incorrect documents are rejected [1].

## 5. Charpak Master’s Scholarship: selection process and typical deadlines

### Selection criteria

Campus France India says applications are first checked for general eligibility, including academic requirements, age limit and required documents [1]. Complete applications are then evaluated on academic excellence, consistency, statement-of-purpose quality and overall profile; there is **no minimum CGPA or percentage** required [1]. Shortlisted applicants are called for an online interview, and the 2026 current page gives an interview window of **13 April to 30 April 2026** [1]. The statement of purpose is subject to plagiarism checking; significant AI-generated content can lead to disqualification [1].

### Typical annual timeline

The exact deadline moves by cycle, and the official Campus France India page retrieved for this report had already moved to a post-results state for 2026, so it did not present an active application deadline. The evidence supports this typical planning window:

| Cycle evidence | Deadline / milestone | Source |
|---|---|---|
| 2025 preserved scholarship-guide evidence | Application deadline reported as 28 February 2025; interviews reported for 7 April – 25 April 2025 | [8][9] |
| Official 2025 result document | Results dated 21 May 2025 | [7] |
| 2026 current official page | Interviews between 13 April and 30 April 2026; results finalized and selected candidates informed by email | [1] |
| 2026 third-party deadline listing | Deadline shown as 30 March 2026 | [10] |

For a Paris-Saclay Economics applicant, the practical ordering is important. The Paris-Saclay M1 Economics window shown on the programme page ran from **15 January to 16 March 2026** [2], while third-party evidence for the 2026 Charpak cycle listed a **30 March 2026** scholarship deadline [10]. In 2025, however, Charpak deadline evidence points to **28 February 2025** [8][9], which could fall before a final admission result. Campus France India explicitly anticipates this situation by permitting email correspondence in place of an acceptance letter if the French institution has not yet issued the letter by the scholarship deadline [1].

## 6. Practical checklist for an Indian/OCI applicant to Paris-Saclay Economics

1. **Confirm academic fit for Paris-Saclay Economics.** The strongest fit is a BA/Licence in economics or BS/Licence in mathematics; for M2, also verify that prior study amounts to M1/240 ECTS or equivalent [2][4][5].
2. **Prepare an English-taught programme file.** Because the programme is in English and English proof may be compulsory for non-English speakers, prepare an English certificate or relevant GMAT/GRE evidence if applicable [2][4].
3. **Apply to Paris-Saclay through the correct route.** Use the programme page dates and platform; M2 applications are through INCEPTION, and Études en France applicants complete the EEF “I am accepted” step after admission [3][5].
4. **Start the Charpak application after applying to Paris-Saclay, not after waiting indefinitely for the final admission letter.** Charpak requires that the applicant has applied to a French institution before applying for the scholarship, and permits proof of ongoing admission correspondence if the acceptance letter is not yet available [1].
5. **Submit one Charpak application only.** If applying to several French programmes, use the top choice in the scholarship form; multiple online applications are disqualifying [1].
6. **Budget beyond the scholarship.** The €860 monthly allowance and fee exemptions are meaningful, but travel and other stay expenses are not covered; differentiated-fee exemption at a public institution must be checked with Paris-Saclay [1].

## Bottom line

As of the retrieved sources, admission to Université Paris-Saclay’s Master’s in Economics is selective, English-taught and primarily aimed at students with economics or mathematics backgrounds, with 50 M1 places and 20 M2 places [2][4][3]. A Charpak Master’s applicant for this programme must independently satisfy the scholarship’s Indian citizen/OCI, age, full-time Master’s, France-based study and prior French-admission-application requirements [1]. The most important timing issue is that the Paris-Saclay admission and Charpak scholarship calendars may overlap; applicants should submit the Paris-Saclay application early and, if the admission letter is not yet available by the Charpak deadline, use the official allowance for proof of ongoing admission correspondence [1].
