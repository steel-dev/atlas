# how to become a nail tech in PA

**Bottom line:** Pennsylvania has a short path, but you must follow the order: finish **200 hours** at a licensed cosmetology school, apply in **PALS**, pass one Pearson VUE exam, then wait for your license before doing paid nail services. If you are comparing schools and wondering **how to become a nail tech in PA**, start with the State Board snapshot: it lists 10th grade education or equivalent, one exam, 200 hours, **no continuing education**, a **$119 initial fee**, and a **$97 renewal fee** [1]. This guide to **how to become a nail tech in PA** keeps the steps simple, but it also points to the exact law so you know what is required. The official external reference is the [Pennsylvania nail technician licensure snapshot](https://www.pa.gov/agencies/dos/department-and-offices/bpoa/boards-commissions/cosmetology/nail-technician-licensure-snapshot) [1].

## Training & Education Requirements

The legal base for **how to become a nail tech in PA** is Pennsylvania Cosmetology Law Act 86. Section 1 defines a “nail technician” and “nail technology,” including manicuring nails, applying artificial or sculptured nails, and massaging hands, lower arms, feet, and lower legs within the statute’s limits [2]. Section 5(b)(2) creates the nail technician limited license and requires **200 hours** in nail technology at a licensed cosmetology school plus a limited exam; it allows testing after 150 hours, but licensure still requires passing, finishing all 200 hours, and school certification [2].

The regulation is just as important. 49 Pa. Code **§ 7.32f** sets exam eligibility at age **16 or older**, 10th grade or equivalent, and 200 school hours, with listed exceptions [3]. 49 Pa. Code **§ 7.129(e)** says the nail technology curriculum must be at least 200 hours, excluding electives, with a recommended split: Professional Practice 25, Sciences 75, Nail Treatments 75, and Pedicuring 25 [4]. Board guidance also says those hours must be earned in not less than one month [5].

## Licensing Steps for Pennsylvania

For most students, **how to become a nail tech in PA** looks like this:

1. Pick a licensed or Board-approved cosmetology school.
2. Complete the required nail technology hours.
3. Apply through **www.pals.pa.gov** and pay the state application fee.
4. Wait for the PALS eligibility letter and Pearson VUE Authorization to Test.
5. Schedule, pay for, and pass the Pearson VUE exam.
6. Receive the license before doing paid services.

Pennsylvania Cosmetology Law § 2 bars paid nail technology, paid teaching, or running a paid nail technology location without the required license [6]. Section 8 generally requires paid nail technology to be practiced in a licensed cosmetology salon or nail-technology-limited salon, while limited license holders may furnish treatments in client residences by appointment; § 8.1 prohibits booth rental by salon owners to license holders under the act [6].

The Pearson VUE Nail Technician Theory/Procedural Skills exam is computer based, with no separate hands-on practical exam. It has **75 total items**: 60 scored and 15 pretest. Time allowed is **1 hour 45 minutes**, and passing means a scaled **75** on a 0–100 scale. The exam is offered in **English, Korean, Spanish, Vietnamese, and Simplified Chinese** [7].

Retakes are allowed during the one-year exam authorization, but each retake costs another **$93** and you must wait 24 hours after failing before reserving again [7].

## FAQs

### How many training hours do I need?
Pennsylvania requires **200 clock hours** of nail technology instruction in a licensed cosmetology school. The state curriculum rule recommends 25 hours of Professional Practice, 75 Sciences, 75 Nail Treatments, and 25 Pedicuring. Full-time training can be about five weeks, while a 24-hour weekly schedule is closer to nine weeks [4] [10].

### What age and education do I need?
For **how to become a nail tech in PA**, the baseline is age **16 or older** and **10th grade or equivalent**. 49 Pa. Code § 7.32f also lists exceptions for certain veterans, applicants age 35 or older, and an Office of Vocational Rehabilitation training alternative [3].

### What exam do Pennsylvania nail techs take?
Pennsylvania uses Pearson VUE for the Nail Technician Theory/Procedural Skills exam. It is computer based, has 75 total items, allows 1 hour 45 minutes, and needs a scaled 75 to pass. There is no separate hands-on practical exam, and the exam is offered in five languages [7].

### How much does the license and exam cost?
The State Board snapshot lists a **$119 initial fee** and **$97 renewal fee**. The initial total is the $26 Pennsylvania application/registration fee plus the $93 Pearson VUE exam fee. Retakes also cost $93 each during the one-year authorization window [1] [7] [8].

### How long does the whole process take?
Training can take about five weeks at 40 hours per week, about nine weeks at 24 hours per week, or roughly three months at 15 hours per week. Add time for the PALS application, eligibility notice, Pearson VUE scheduling, exam results, and final license issuance after passing.

### When do I renew my PA nail technician license?
Nail technician licenses expire **January 31 of even-numbered years** and renew for **$97**. Pennsylvania lists no continuing education requirement. If you miss the deadline, the license becomes unregistered and you may not practice; working unregistered can add a **$5-per-month or part-month** penalty plus possible discipline or criminal prosecution [1] [15] [16].

### What if my PA nail technician license has been expired?
If the license has been expired less than five years, Pennsylvania directs nail technicians to reactivate through PALS. If it has been expired more than five years, the person must retake the examination to reactivate. That is separate from ordinary biennial renewal by January 31 of even-numbered years [15].

### Is the job outlook good?
Nationally, BLS projects Manicurists and Pedicurists to grow **7% from 2024 to 2034**, with about **24,800 openings per year**. Pennsylvania pay estimates are lower than the national median, so students should compare local salons, tip potential, schedules, and commuting costs before choosing a program [13] [14].

## Conclusion

Now you know **how to become a nail tech in PA**: complete 200 school hours, apply through PALS, pass Pearson VUE, receive the license, work only in allowed settings, and renew on time. The fastest training path is a little over one month, but many students should plan on two to three months plus application and exam time.
