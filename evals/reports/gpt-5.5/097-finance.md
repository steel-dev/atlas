## Cash generation efficiency

CME’s operating cash flow increased from **$892.7 million** in Q1 2024 to **$1,116.6 million** in Q1 2025, a gain of **$223.9 million**, or **25.1%**. Over the same period, net income increased from **$855.2 million** to **$956.2 million**, a gain of **$101.0 million**, or **11.8%** [1]. The result is a clear improvement in cash conversion: **104.4%** in Q1 2024 versus **116.8%** in Q1 2025, using net cash provided by operating activities divided by net income [1].

The working-capital detail shows why cash conversion improved even with a larger receivables build:

| Cash-flow component | Q1 2025 | Q1 2024 | Change | Effect on cash-generation read |
|---|---:|---:|---:|---|
| Accounts receivable | **$(196.8)m** | **$(93.4)m** | **$(103.4)m** | Larger receivable growth consumed cash; this is the main working-capital drag in the comparison [1]. |
| Income taxes payable | **$280.9m** | **$151.4m** | **+$129.5m** | Larger tax payable accrual was a cash source and more than offset the additional receivables drag [1]. |
| Net cash provided by operating activities | **$1,116.6m** | **$892.7m** | **+$223.9m** | OCF growth exceeded net-income growth, lifting conversion [1]. |

The receivables movement points to stronger activity or revenue timing, but it did not weaken overall cash-generation efficiency because the larger tax-payable source and earnings growth lifted operating cash flow above net income. On the stated conversion measure, CME generated **$1.17 of operating cash flow per $1.00 of net income** in Q1 2025, compared with **$1.04** in Q1 2024 [1].

## Overall assessment

CME’s capital structure supports sustainable growth while maintaining financial flexibility for strategic investments.
