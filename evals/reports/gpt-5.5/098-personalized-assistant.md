## Decision frame applied

The comparison is framed around the stated situation: a **52-year-old corporate accountant in Melbourne**, current gross income **AUD 145,000/year**, intended move within **18 months of 2026-06-23**, planned retirement at **age 60**, and a semi-retirement income target of **at least AUD 35/hour**. ## Weighted ranking and recommendation

### 1) Toowoomba — recommended first choice

Toowoomba gives the best retirement-transition balance. Its tested monthly basket is **AUD 2,552.48**, saving **AUD 1,780.90/month (-41.1%)** versus Melbourne, the largest saving of the three [1][2]. The local job signal is strong enough for the AUD 35/hour target: **78** Jora part-time accounting ads and a visible qualifying part-time bookkeeping role at **AUD 36–38/hour**, with remote Queensland fallback roles visible at **AUD 36–40/hour** and **AUD 45–55/hour** [3][15]. The healthcare case is the strongest, with St Andrew’s Toowoomba Hospital’s 24/7 cardiac emergency service, cath labs and EP/ablation material, plus private cardiology clinics listing Holter, echo, ECG, cardioversion and GP-referral pathways [4][5][6][16].

The main weakness is 3-bedroom purchase affordability: the Toowoomba SA3 3-bedroom median is **AUD 634,000** in Jun-25, **AUD 84,000 above** the AUD 550,000 target, with a reported five-year increase of **+14.0% p.a.** [14]. Toowoomba remains the top recommendation only if you are prepared to buy below median, consider older stock or surrounding suburbs, or rent first while searching.

### 2) Cairns — best fallback if paid work is the gating issue

Cairns has the best visible local pay signal: **75** part-time accounting ads and visible local roles at **AUD 40/hour**, **AUD 35–40/hour**, and **AUD 40–45/hour**, giving an ordinary visible part-time accounting/bookkeeping rate around **AUD 40/hour** [8]. Its healthcare profile is also solid for a regional centre, with evidence of Cairns Hospital cardiology, cath-lab capacity, local private cardiology, echo and Holter services. The trade-off is cost: the tested basket is **AUD 3,598.63/month**, only **AUD 734.75/month (-17.0%)** cheaper than Melbourne, and the Cairns SA4 3-bedroom median is **AUD 609,750** in Jun-25, **AUD 59,750 above** the AUD 550,000 target [13][1][11]. Choose Cairns over Toowoomba if you find a better part-time role there before moving or if your cardiologist prefers the Cairns/Townsville/Brisbane referral network for your specific AF history.

### 3) Bundaberg — best affordability fallback, weakest income sustainability

Bundaberg’s combined basket is **AUD 2,769.05/month**, saving **AUD 1,564.33/month (-36.1%)** versus Melbourne, and its fetched 3-bedroom median is the lowest of the three at **AUD 584,200** in Jun-25, only **AUD 34,200 above** the AUD 550,000 target [12][1][10]. That makes Bundaberg the best branch if the overriding goal is to buy as close as possible to AUD 550,000 while still materially cutting monthly costs.

The downside is work risk. The local Jora page shows only **18** part-time accounting ads, and the visible local hourly-rate evidence at or above **AUD 35/hour** is not reliable enough to establish a robust median; the remote Queensland fallback is therefore central, not optional [9][15]. Bundaberg’s named provider set suggests adequate routine cardiology and acute coronary capability through Bundaberg Hospital/Bundaberg Base Hospital, Advara HeartCare and The Friendlies, but for AF rhythm procedures it is more likely to depend on referral to Sunshine Coast or Brisbane tertiary centres than Toowoomba is.

## Branch rule: when healthcare should override cost savings

If your Melbourne cardiologist considers your atrial fibrillation stable, with routine anticoagulation and rate/rhythm-control follow-up only, **Toowoomba** is the preferred move because it combines the largest monthly saving with the clearest local cardiology and emergency cardiac-care evidence. If you have recent symptomatic episodes, medication changes, suspected need for ablation, complex anticoagulation issues, or a history of rapid AF requiring emergency care, select the town only after confirming named cardiologist availability and wait times; in that branch, healthcare access should override a small cost difference, which favours **Toowoomba first** and **Cairns second**, not Bundaberg.

Before moving, arrange a GP and cardiologist handover/referral, keep anticoagulation and rate/rhythm-control medicines exactly as prescribed unless changed by a clinician, and confirm Medicare/private-cover access and cardiology wait times in the chosen town. Seek emergency care immediately or call 000 for chest pain, fainting, stroke symptoms, severe breathlessness, or sustained rapid/irregular palpitations; St Andrew’s emergency-heart material explicitly treats chest pain/suspected heart attack, irregular rhythms, sudden breathlessness, palpitations, dizziness and fainting as urgent cardiac presentations [4].
