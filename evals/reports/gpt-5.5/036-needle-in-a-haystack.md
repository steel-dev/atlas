# Answer: Red Rooster’s **Quarter Chicken Lunch**

The chicken chain in the clue is **Red Rooster**, and the promotion the clue is pointing to is best identified as the **Quarter Chicken Lunch**. Around 2015, the same lunch-offer format was advertised as a Red Rooster lunch deal of **quarter chicken + small chips for $5**, available **11am–4pm daily**; in later Red Rooster-linked deal listings the same core offer is explicitly named **“$5 Quarter Chicken Lunch”** and described as being available until 4pm [1][2][3][4].

## Why the chain is Red Rooster

The biographical and brand clues line up closely with Red Rooster:

| Clue element | Evidence |
|---|---|
| Perth-suburban origin in 1972 | Red Rooster is described as founded/opened in **1972** at **Kelmscott**, a Perth suburb in Western Australia [5][6][7]. |
| Kailis / Greek-family connection | The founding story is tied to **Peter Kailis** and the Kailis family. Sources describe Peter Kailis as a son of Greek migrant George Kailis/Kallis, and one account says Peter and brother Theo, with partners, launched the first Red Rooster by acquiring an existing Kelmscott chicken shop [5][6]. |
| Roast-chicken identity | Red Rooster’s own site says the brand specialises in roast and fried chicken, and Business News describes Red Rooster as a Perth-founded quick-service restaurant that “serves roast chicken” [8][7]. |

So the “red signage / roast chicken / Perth suburb / Greek-Australian brothers-family” clue is not pointing to a generic chicken shop; it identifies **Red Rooster**.

## The promotion around 2015

The closest contemporaneous evidence found for “around 2015” is a 2015 Frugal Feeds deal post headed **“Red Rooster – Quarter Chicken & Small Chips for $5.”** It states that Red Rooster was offering “a lunch deal with a quarter chicken and a small chips for just $5,” and that it was “only available from **11am to 4pm daily**” for a limited time [1]. That matches the user’s description on all the functional details: a portion of chicken, chips, a few dollars, and a mid-afternoon cutoff.

## Why the answer is a promotion name, not just “quarter chicken and chips”

The evidence distinguishes the queried item from an ordinary menu description in two ways.

First, the 2015 item is explicitly framed as a **lunch deal**, not merely a standing menu line: it was “for just $5,” “only available from 11am to 4pm daily,” and “available for a limited time only” [1]. Ordinary quarter chicken plus chips would not need a daily lunch window or limited-time terms.

Second, later sources attach an explicit promotion label to the same pattern. In 2020 and 2022, Frugal Feeds headlines call it **“$5 Quarter Chicken Lunch,”** and the 2021 Red Rooster-linked OzBargain post calls it **“$5 Quarter Chicken Lunch”** / “Quarter Chicken Lunch Meal,” with terms limiting it to in-restaurant/drive-thru and until 4pm [2][3][4]. That makes **Quarter Chicken Lunch** the promotion name to give as the answer, while the 2015 wording supplies the exact around-2015 contents, price, and 11am–4pm availability.

## Variations over time

The promotion appears to have kept a stable core—**quarter roast chicken plus chips at lunchtime, with a 4pm cutoff**—while the bundle and wording varied:

- **2015:** quarter chicken + small chips, **$5**, **11am–4pm daily** [1].
- **2017:** quarter chicken + small chips + 250ml Coke, “from **$5**,” under the heading **Quarter Chicken Deal** [9].
- **2020–2022:** **Quarter Chicken Lunch**, generally quarter roast chicken + small chips + mash & gravy, **$5** in many regions, until **4pm** [2][3][4].

Thus, for “around 2015,” the precise contemporaneous offer was **Quarter Chicken & Small Chips for $5**; the recurring/branded name evidenced in later Red Rooster-linked deal material is **Quarter Chicken Lunch**. If the question asks “What’s the promotion called?”, the best concise answer is **Red Rooster’s Quarter Chicken Lunch**.

## Evidence for a 1990s predecessor

The fetched sources did not produce an official 1990s Red Rooster advertisement or menu using the exact modern name **Quarter Chicken Lunch**. The available evidence for a 1990s materially similar promotion is later consumer recollection, mainly on OzBargain. In a 2014 Red Rooster deal thread, users recall **“$2 Tuesday’s quarter chicken and chips at Red Roster”** [sic] and another user dates it to “about 22 years ago,” i.e. roughly the early 1990s, before it became “half price Monday and Tuesday” chicken and chips [10]. In a 2017 Red Rooster quarter-chicken deal thread, users again recall **“$2 chicken and chips”** from primary-school days about 25 years earlier, and a former worker says customers bought “$2 chicken and chips on Monday and Tuesday” and that the promo “ran for years” [9].

That evidence is weaker than an archived official ad: it supports that a **materially similar Red Rooster quarter-chicken/chips value promotion existed in the 1990s**, but it does not prove that the exact modern name **Quarter Chicken Lunch** was already in use then. The strongest conclusion is therefore:

> The chain was **Red Rooster**. The queried lunchtime deal is **Quarter Chicken Lunch**; around 2015 it appeared as a **$5 quarter chicken and small chips lunch deal**, available **11am–4pm daily**, and later Red Rooster-linked material names the recurring version **$5 Quarter Chicken Lunch** [1][2][3][4].
