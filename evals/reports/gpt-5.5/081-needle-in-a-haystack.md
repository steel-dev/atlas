## 5. Offer-fit comparison and weak fits

The **SuperMoney cluster** is coherent because all five Miro labels are consumer comparison categories: credit cards, personal loans, auto-loan refinancing, student-loan refinancing, and debt-consolidation loans. SuperMoney’s stated partner tools — tracked links, embedded product listings, widgets, publisher network, prequalification platform, and co-branded offer engines — are designed for exactly that kind of content page or calculator-style funnel [19]. The main limitation is economic transparency: SuperMoney publicly confirms commissions but not the exact payout schedule or conversion event in its public affiliate/help pages [19] [20].

The **Novae cluster** is also coherent, but it is more reseller/referral-oriented than pure comparison-shopping. Debt Help, Business Debt Help, Business Credit Builder, Wills/Trusts, and Life Insurance sit inside Novae’s partner ecosystem, where Novae either supplies a branded product/platform itself — especially business credit and credit-monitoring/dispute tools — or routes the user to a named third-party partner such as Trust & Will or Policygenius [29] [25] [26] [27]. This fits a credit-repair/business-funding consultancy because debt relief, business-credit building, and credit-monitoring tools are natural cross-sells to customers seeking credit restoration or business capital.

The weakest fits are not contradictions, but distinctions Mesa must keep clear in page copy: **debt consolidation loan** is a SuperMoney loan-comparison product, whereas **debt relief** and **business debt relief** are Novae debt-resolution/restructuring programs with fee, credit-impact, and state-availability disclosures [18] [24] [28]. Likewise, **Trust & Will** and **Policygenius** are third-party partner flows, not Novae-authored estate documents or Novae-issued insurance policies [25] [26] [27].

## 6. Compliance implications for a Mesa-style affiliate funnel

A Mesa funnel promoting these offers should treat disclosures as part of the product page, not as fine print:

- **FTC Endorsement Guides, 16 CFR Part 255.** Affiliate links, paid referrals, co-brand/white-label compensation, and testimonials require clear and conspicuous disclosure of material connections, especially where Mesa earns commissions or recurring revenue from SuperMoney, Novae, SmartCredit, Trust & Will, Policygenius, or other partners.
- **Credit Repair Organizations Act, 15 U.S.C. §§ 1679–1679j.** Credit-repair and credit-restoration claims must avoid misleading guarantees and must account for CROA’s written-contract, cancellation-right, and advance-fee restrictions for credit-repair organizations.
- **Telemarketing Sales Rule, 16 CFR Part 310.** Debt-relief marketing must account for the TSR’s debt-relief advance-fee restrictions; Novae’s own Debt Help page says it complies with 16 C.F.R. Part 310 and does not charge upfront fees, but Mesa still needs consistent page, phone, SMS, and call-center practices [24].
- **Loan, credit-card, and insurance disclosures.** Mesa should avoid implying that it, SuperMoney, or Novae approves credit, issues credit cards, underwrites insurance, or guarantees funding. SuperMoney’s terms place credit decisions with third-party Providers, and Mesa’s business-credit page similarly says Mesa/Mesa Group Capital are not lenders, banks, credit bureaus, or financial institutions and that issuing banks/lenders make credit decisions [11] [10]. For life insurance, Mesa should identify Policygenius/licensed agents as the insurance path rather than presenting Mesa or Novae as the insurer [27].

## Conclusion

Evert is most likely leveraging **SuperMoney** as an affiliate monetization layer for financial comparison pages and **Novae** as a partner/reseller/referral platform for credit, debt, business-credit, estate-planning, and insurance pages. The SuperMoney pages are best understood as tracked comparison/offers traffic; the Novae pages are best understood as co-branded, white-label, or affiliate/reseller services where Novae or named partners perform the underlying work. Mesa’s role is the funnel owner and consultant-facing brand, not the originator of the loans, credit cards, debt-relief settlements, estate plans, or insurance policies.
