# Industrial IoT predictive-maintenance sensor platform choice for a 50 km² Australian mine — Siemens vs Honeywell vs MOVUS vs DINGO/local integrator

**Bottom line.** For an extreme-heat/dust Australian mining site, **Honeywell ranks first overall** if the site needs hazardous-area readiness and long-range field networking because Versatilis is IP66/IP67, -40 to +80 °C, IECEx/ATEX Ex ia IIB T4 Ga, and uses LoRaWAN with 20 days local data logging, while OneWireless provides ISA100/WirelessHART/LoRaWAN, OPC UA/DA, Modbus TCP/RTU, MQTT and native Experion PKS CDA integration [1][2]. **Siemens ranks first only where the installed base is SIMATIC/WinCC/PROFINET/S7**, because MS200 is rugged at IP69 and -30 to +80 °C but the standard MS200/CC220/SCM IQ source does not state IECEx/ATEX for MS200 and CC220 is IP20, 0 to +50 °C and cabinet-mounted [3]. **MOVUS is the fastest local retrofit option**—Brisbane-origin FitMachine is IP66, -20 to +60 °C, Wi-Fi/BLE, REST/API-integrated, with Australian mining references—but the public sources do not establish native OPC UA/MQTT, exact offline-buffering duration, current sensing, or LoRa [4][5][6]. **DINGO/Trakka should be treated as a mining asset-health software layer/local integrator option, not a sensor platform**, because the sourced material describes ingestion of existing condition-monitoring, onboard-sensor, ERP, CMMS and mobile/field-device data rather than a directly specified DINGO rugged sensor [7][8].

## 2. Required vs optional procurement envelope

**Minimum requirements for this mine:**

- **Dust/water protection:** require **IP6X dust-tight and IP66 or better** at the field sensor. IEC 60529 first digit 6 is dust-tight/no dust ingress; IP66 protects against powerful water jets; IP67 adds temporary immersion; IP68 is manufacturer-defined continuous immersion, so the depth/time must be stated by the vendor [19].
- **Temperature:** require **certified operating maximum above the site design ambient**, not storage temperature. For 50 °C-plus Australian ambient, allow enclosure solar loading and derating; field sensors at +60 °C or +80 °C are materially stronger than IP20 gateways rated only to +50 °C [3][4][1].
- **SCADA/historian integration:** require OPC UA/DA, Modbus TCP/RTU, MQTT, REST or a historian connector matching the installed estate; for Siemens, the sourced connectors include OPC UA, Modbus TCP, SIMATIC S7, PROFINET IO and MQTT/REST through OPC Router; for Honeywell WDM, Modbus, OPC UA, OPC DA proxy, MQTT and Experion PKS CDA are explicit [10][2].
- **Edge buffering:** require field or gateway buffering adequate for planned communications outages. Honeywell Versatilis states **20 days** local data logging at default configuration [1]. MOVUS public sources state Wi-Fi/cellular gateway operation and MachineCloud integration but not a precise offline-buffer duration [20][12]. Siemens MS200/CC220 sources state polling and MQTT upload but not an offline-buffer duration [3].
- **Cyber hardening:** require encryption, identity/access control, network segmentation and patch/firmware management. Honeywell has the strongest sourced protocol/security specifics; MOVUS gives SaaS encryption and RBAC; Siemens edge security must be confirmed in the selected Industrial Edge deployment [13][12][10].
- **Hazardous areas:** require **IECEx-certified equipment where the inspection dossier classifies gas or combustible dust risk**; FitMachine EX and Versatilis have explicit IECEx/ATEX markings in the fetched specs, while standard Siemens MS200/CC220/SCM IQ source does not [9][1][3].

**Optional features:** cloud AI dashboards, digital twins, advanced fleet analytics and enterprise maintenance optimisation are valuable but should not substitute for IP6X/IP66+, temperature rating, hazardous-area certification where applicable, local buffering, SCADA/historian integration and cybersecurity.

## 4. Edge stack and SCADA integration

### Siemens

**SIMATIC IOT2050 hardware.** The IOT2050 Advance 6ES7647-0BA00-1YA2 has **ARM TI AM6548 HS**, **2 GB DDR4 RAM**, **16 GB eMMC**, 2 × Gbit Ethernet RJ45, DisplayPort, 2 × USB 2.0, microSD slot and 12/24 V DC supply; the datasheet states **IP20** and **0..+50 °C** operation [22]. The family manual lists Basic as TI AM6528 dual-core/1 GB RAM/no eMMC, Advance and M.2 as TI AM6548 HS quad-core/2 GB RAM/16 GB eMMC, and SM as TI AM6548 HS quad-core/4 GB RAM/16 GB eMMC; non-SM mounting limits are 0..50 °C vertical DIN rail, 0..40 °C horizontal DIN rail, 0..45 °C vertical wall and 0..40 °C horizontal wall, while SM is -20..50 °C vertical DIN rail [23].

**Local analytics and protocols.** Siemens Industrial Edge runs edge apps/connectors locally on the device. The sourced Industrial Edge connectivity list includes Siemens Connectivity Suite connectors for **OPC UA**, **Modbus TCP**, **SIMATIC S7** and **PROFINET IO**, plus OPC Router Enterprise/Standard connectors for **MQTT broker**, AWS/Azure IoT MQTT, Sparkplug MQTT, REST APIs/clients over HTTP/HTTPS, databases including MSSQL/PostgreSQL/Oracle/MySQL/Access, SAP, Kafka and TCP [10]. For WinCC/PCS 7/third-party SCADA, the practical integration route is therefore OPC UA/Modbus TCP/S7/PROFINET/MQTT via licensed Industrial Edge/connectivity applications; exact per-connector licence SKUs and WinCC/PCS 7 certified connector licences must be confirmed in the Siemens quote because the public connectivity page identifies connectors, not commercial licensing [10].

**Constraint for this mine.** MS200 is field-rugged and hot-rated, but the CC220 and IOT2050 are IP20 and +50 °C-class cabinet devices, so they require shaded, dust-sealed, cooled industrial enclosures for outdoor deployment [3][22].

### Honeywell

**Versatilis/Experion EHM edge stack.** Versatilis uses BLE 5.0 plus LoRaWAN Class A and can log **20 days** of data at default configuration [1]. Honeywell Versatilis Experion Equipment Health Monitoring is an on-premises end-to-end monitoring architecture for Versatilis: transmitters are configured by the Versatilis Connect BLE app for a LoRaWAN region; a commercial LoRaWAN gateway feeds an on-prem PC running two VMs; ChirpStack deciphers payloads and sends data by MQTT to the Experion EHM VM, with example ChirpStack VM requirements of **2 CPU cores, 6 GB RAM and 60 GB disk** [15][24]. Product functions include site/asset/transmitter hierarchy, dashboard overview, historisation and measurement alarms, transmitter drill-down, ISO-based asset health reports and transmitter diagnostics; additional licensed features add advanced vibration analytics for fault verification/alerts and asset-health report generation [15].

**OneWireless gateway and SCADA.** OneWireless WDM is the strongest sourced SCADA integration stack in this comparison: it supports ISA100 Wireless IEC 62734, WirelessHART IEC 62591, LoRaWAN instruments, Wi-Fi and Ethernet/IP devices; translates to **Modbus RTU/Serial and TCP/IP**, HART RTU/IP, **OPC UA**, **OPC DA via supplied OPC-UA-to-OPC-DA proxy**, **MQTT**, Honeywell **Experion PKS CDA**, GCI and Enraf; and supports up to 40 FDAP/PCAP access points, 60 field-device routers, 80 total AP+routers, 500 ISA100/WirelessHART instruments and 500 LoRaWAN instruments depending on update rates [2]. For SCADA/network engineering, WDM redundancy has a typical control-processing switchover of **15 s** and maximum initial synchronization of **180 s**, and the WDM alarm/event store is **1,000 alarms** and **24,576 events** [2]. Experion PKS integration via WDM CDA lets C300 controllers and ACE nodes communicate natively with wireless field devices; OneWireless FDAP can also be configured as **Field Expandable Wireless IO (FEWIO)** acting as a **Modbus slave** to transmit Modbus RTU/TCP devices over ISA100 Wireless [13].

**Cyber/security and analytics scope.** OneWireless security includes ISA Secure Level 1 certification, AES-128 process-data encryption, WPA/WPA2/802.11i, 802.1X/EAP options, VPN/IPsec/L2TP and configurable key rotation; Versatilis adds AES-256 LoRa/BLE encryption [13][1]. The sourced Honeywell predictive-maintenance edge path in this report is **Versatilis + on-prem Experion EHM + OneWireless/WDM**, not a Forge-priced architecture: edge/on-prem analytics, historisation and measurement alarms sit in Experion EHM, while SCADA/historian integration is through WDM protocols such as OPC UA/DA proxy, Modbus, MQTT and Experion PKS CDA [15][2]. Exact Forge Performance+ or Forge Production Intelligence licence pricing and historian connector licensing are therefore not assumed; the cost model uses a budgetary Honeywell software allowance rather than a quoted Forge SKU.

### MOVUS and local-provider stack

**FitMachine architecture.** FitMachine communicates by **2.4 GHz Wi-Fi** to customer Wi-Fi or a FitMachine Gateway and by Bluetooth for smartphone onboarding; MOVUS FAQ specifies RSSI **-70 dBm or better** and says a MOVUS cellular Wi-Fi gateway can be used where cellular coverage exists [4][12]. The FitMachine Gateway datasheet lists Intel Quark X1021D 400 MHz, **1 GB DDR3**, **8 GB eMMC**, Linux/OpenWRT, 85–264 VAC or PoE, optional 10/100 Ethernet, 2 × RS-232, USB, Wi-Fi 802.11ac/a/b/g/n, Bluetooth 4.0/BLE, 4G with 3G fallback, GPS, optional Digi XBee/XBee-PRO and **-10..+60 °C** operation [20]. Each MOVUS sensor transmits about **1–2 MB/day** [12].

**Analytics and integrations.** MOVUS MachineCloud API uses HTTPS/JSON for registering/updating FitMachines, permissions/notifications and reading samples/events/status/condition; MachineCloud Streams deliver real-time condition data, sample data and alarm events; integration references include RESTful API, OSIsoft PI Historian, IBM Maximo and SAP HANA, plus REST API and webhooks in the FAQ [5][11][12]. Security controls include TLS 1.2 in transit, AES-256 at rest, role-based access control, API/auth monitoring, multi-tenant SaaS separation and AWS Sydney data centres [12].

**Protocol limitations for legacy SCADA.** Public MOVUS sources establish REST/API/webhooks/streams and PI/Maximo/SAP HANA references; they do **not** establish native OPC UA, native MQTT, native LoRa, AVEVA/Wonderware, GE iFIX, Ignition or Citect/AVEVA Plant SCADA certified connectors, nor an exact offline-buffering duration or exact edge-inference location [5][11][12]. If SCADA hard integration is mandatory, specify an intermediate OPC UA/MQTT gateway or PI connector in the integrator scope.

### DINGO/Trakka integration role

DINGO Trakka is best scoped as the enterprise condition-intelligence layer over multiple sources. Its sourced pages describe collection and analysis of condition-monitoring data, onboard sensors, ERP, CMMS, mobile and field-device data, with a preconfigured connector library and direct or parallel ERP integration [7]. The public pages do not identify exact AVEVA/Wonderware, GE iFIX, Ignition, Citect/AVEVA Plant SCADA, OSIsoft PI/AVEVA PI, OPC UA, MQTT or REST connector SKUs, so those must be contract deliverables if DINGO/local integrator is selected [7][8].

## 5. Durability, hazardous-area compliance and exclusion test

**Durability standards.** For dust and washdown, field sensors should be IP6X and IP66+. NEMA 4X and NEMA 6 cannot be treated as simple equivalents to IP ratings: NEMA’s own guide provides only NEMA-to-IP conversion guidance and says it cannot be used to convert IEC IP ratings to NEMA ratings, because NEMA includes tests such as corrosion, gasket aging and external icing that IEC IP does not [19].

**Australian hazardous-area compliance.** In Australian mines, gas and combustible-dust hazardous areas should use equipment certified to the applicable IECEx/AS/NZS 60079 equipment protection concept and the mine’s hazardous-area classification; the NSW Resources Regulator states that Work Health and Safety (Mines and Petroleum Sites) Regulation 2022 **section 81** requires plant used in an underground coal-mine hazardous zone to be explosion-protected and, for electrical plant, to have a suitable explosion-protection level and valid certificate of conformity or be on the Explosion Protected Electrical Apparatus Approvals List [25]. In practical procurement terms: require the IECEx certificate number, Ex marking, equipment protection level, gas/dust group, T-class or max surface temperature, IP rating and Tamb range on the purchase schedule. FitMachine EX and Versatilis provide explicit Ex markings in the fetched specs; Siemens MS200/CC220/SCM IQ does not in the fetched Siemens source [9][1][3].

**Network implication:** Honeywell has the most practical single-vendor 50 km² wireless story because Versatilis is LoRaWAN and OneWireless can also handle ISA100/WirelessHART/Wi-Fi/Ethernet. Siemens MS200’s BLE-to-CC220 limit of eight sensors per CC220 is acceptable inside plants but costly across a dispersed 50 km² mine. MOVUS is practical for rapid deployment on clustered assets but not for blanket 50 km² Wi-Fi coverage.

## 8. Choose-when logic

- **Choose Siemens** when the mine already runs a dominant **SIMATIC/WinCC/PCS 7/PROFINET/S7** estate, the target assets are in fixed plant with cabinets/network drops nearby, and hazardous-area zones are either absent or can be covered by a certified third-party sensor feeding Siemens Industrial Edge via OPC UA/MQTT. Siemens’ best evidence is MS200’s **IP69, -30..+80 °C** field rating and Industrial Edge’s OPC UA/Modbus TCP/S7/PROFINET/MQTT/REST connector ecosystem [3][10].
- **Choose Honeywell** when the mine already has **Experion PKS, OneWireless or ISA100/WirelessHART**, or when the procurement priority is a single industrial wireless stack with hazardous-area-capable sensors, on-prem analytics and SCADA protocols. Honeywell’s best evidence is Versatilis **IP66/IP67, -40..+80 °C, IECEx/ATEX Ex ia IIB T4 Ga, 5-year battery and 20-day logging**, plus WDM’s OPC UA/DA, Modbus, MQTT and Experion CDA support [1][2].
- **Choose MOVUS/local** when the priority is **rapid retrofit**, Australian support and condition alerts for clustered fixed assets rather than deep DCS integration. MOVUS’ best evidence is FitMachine **IP66, -20..+60 °C**, magnetic rapid deployment, MachineCloud REST/API/streams, AWS Sydney data storage and Central Queensland coal mine proof with **AUD 1.7M** avoided lost production [4][5][6][12].
- **Choose DINGO/local integrator** when the mine wants a multi-vendor asset-health operating model across existing vibration routes, onboard equipment sensors, ERP, CMMS and field-device data. DINGO’s best evidence is Trakka’s connector/library positioning, >150,000 components managed daily, 250+ operations across six continents and open-cut coal/gold-miner references [7][8].

## 9. Procurement specification to issue

For the RFP, specify the platform as follows:

1. **Field sensors:** IP6X/IP66 minimum, certified operating range at least **site design ambient + 10 °C** or vendor-confirmed derating analysis; vibration and temperature minimum; acoustic and pressure/humidity optional; current sensing only if the vendor provides a sourced sensor variant.
2. **Hazardous areas:** IECEx certificate, Ex marking, gas/dust group, EPL, T-class/max surface temperature, IP rating and Tamb range mandatory for classified zones. Versatilis is IIB gas; FitMachine EX covers IIC gas and IIIC dust; Siemens standard MS200 package needs a certificate or a third-party hazardous-area sensor substitution [9][1][3].
3. **Edge/offline:** minimum buffered data retention equal to the mine’s communications outage design case; Honeywell’s sourced value is **20 days**, while Siemens and MOVUS require vendor confirmation or an added edge store-and-forward gateway [1][3][12].
4. **Integration:** OPC UA and MQTT at the mine DMZ, historian write-back to OSIsoft PI/AVEVA PI or equivalent, and SCADA read-only tag exposure. Honeywell and Siemens meet this most directly; MOVUS and DINGO should include an integration gateway/connector deliverable [2][10][5].
5. **50 km² network:** do not buy a blanket Wi-Fi sensor architecture for the whole mine. Use LoRaWAN/ISA100/private LTE/fibre backhaul for dispersed assets, and reserve Wi-Fi/BLE products for clustered plant areas.
6. **Commercials:** require Australian delivered unit prices for sensors, gateways, edge nodes, software licences, subscriptions, installation, commissioning, SCADA/historian integration, training, spares and five-year support; public distributor pricing should be treated only as a pre-tender budget check [16][17][18][14].

**Recommended award path:** shortlist **Honeywell** as the technical baseline; include **MOVUS** as a rapid-retrofit/local-support alternative for non-DCS-critical assets; keep **Siemens** in contention where the installed automation base is Siemens-heavy; and use **DINGO/local integrator** either as the asset-health overlay or as the fallback integration architecture when a preferred sensor vendor fails temperature, IP, hazardous-area, buffering, protocol or 50 km² network economics constraints.
