# Fortive portfolio strategy after the Precision Technologies / Ralliant separation

## Executive conclusion

Fortive completed the separation of its former Precision Technologies segment into **Ralliant Corporation** on **June 28, 2025**, through a 100% pro rata spin-off of Ralliant shares to Fortive shareholders: **one Ralliant common share for every three Fortive common shares** held on the June 16, 2025 record date; Ralliant began regular-way NYSE trading on June 30, 2025 under ticker **RAL** [1] [2]. The separation changed Fortive’s portfolio from a three-segment company — **Intelligent Operating Solutions (IOS), Precision Technologies (PT), and Advanced Healthcare Solutions (AHS)** — into a post-spin Fortive composed of **IOS + AHS**, while Ralliant holds the former PT businesses [3].

The segment economics show a **mixed but strategically coherent** portfolio move. On the comparable Fortive pre-spin segment-operating-profit basis, **IOS has the strongest absolute unit economics**: **24.67% Q2 2025 operating margin** versus **17.80% for Precision/Ralliant** and **12.24% for AHS**. However, **AHS has the best margin trajectory** versus full-year 2024, expanding slightly by **+15 bps**, while IOS declined **-128 bps** and Precision/Ralliant declined **-463 bps**. Revenue momentum also favors the retained portfolio: from 2023 to 2024, AHS grew **+4.74%**, IOS grew **+3.92%**, and Precision/Ralliant grew only **+0.26%** [4] [3].

The evidence therefore supports Fortive’s stated rationale — a tighter portfolio focused on recurring, workflow, productivity, safety, and healthcare operations businesses — but it does not yet prove financial strengthening at the consolidated level. Fortive’s total-company GAAP operating margin fell from **19.82% in Q1 2024** to **15.85% in Q1 2025**, a **-398 bps** decline, before the separation was reflected as discontinued operations [5]. The post-separation portfolio looks **strategically more focused and, on Q2 2025 run-rate segment margins, somewhat cleaner**, but the near-term financial conclusion is **mixed** because Fortive gives up a historically high-profit PT earnings stream and retains AHS, which is faster-growing but structurally lower-margin.

## Basis of comparison and reporting reconciliation

The central comparability issue is presentation. Fortive’s Q2 2025 release states that Ralliant would be classified as a discontinued operation beginning in the third quarter of 2025. It also states that the results of the Precision Technologies segment are included in Fortive consolidated results for Q2 2025 and Q2 2024, while figures labeled “Fortive Continuing Operations” assume Precision/Ralliant had been treated as discontinued operations for those prior periods [2]. Fortive’s Q2 2025 Form 10-Q still reports the three segments — IOS, PT, and AHS — for the quarter ended June 27, 2025, because the distribution occurred on June 28, 2025, after the quarter-end reporting period [3].

For that reason, the operating-margin comparisons below use Fortive’s **pre-spin three-segment segment table** for Q2 2025 and Fortive’s FY2024 segment table. This is the most apples-to-apples basis for comparing IOS, Precision/Ralliant, and AHS. A separate post-spin Ralliant standalone Q2 2025 basis exists — Ralliant reported Q2 2025 sales of **$503.3 million** and operating profit of **$58.9 million**, or **11.7%** margin — but that includes Ralliant’s standalone public-company structure and is not the same as Fortive’s pre-spin PT segment table of **$523.6 million** sales and **$93.2 million** segment operating profit [6].

All segment margins in this report are calculated as:

> **Segment operating margin = segment operating profit ÷ segment sales**

This is Fortive’s management-defined **segment operating profit** basis. Segment operating profit excludes “Other” unallocated corporate costs and is therefore not the same as consolidated GAAP operating income; it is also not adjusted operating profit. By contrast, the Q1 total-company margin comparison uses **GAAP consolidated operating profit ÷ total sales**, including corporate/unallocated effects [4] [3].

## Segment operating margins: Q2 2025 versus FY2024

| Segment | Q2 2025 sales | Q2 2025 segment operating profit | Q2 2025 margin | FY2024 sales | FY2024 segment operating profit | FY2024 margin | Q2 2025 vs FY2024 margin change |
|---|---:|---:|---:|---:|---:|---:|---:|
| Intelligent Operating Solutions | $675.7M | $166.7M | **24.67%** | $2,714.7M | $704.6M | **25.95%** | **-128 bps** |
| Precision Technologies / Ralliant | $523.6M | $93.2M | **17.80%** | $2,229.4M | $500.0M | **22.43%** | **-463 bps** |
| Advanced Healthcare Solutions | $319.5M | $39.1M | **12.24%** | $1,287.7M | $155.6M | **12.08%** | **+15 bps** |

Sources: Fortive’s Q2 2025 three-segment table for Q2 sales and operating profit [3]; Fortive’s FY2024 segment table for annual sales and operating profit [4]. Margins and basis-point changes are calculated from those source figures.

**Ranking by absolute unit economics:**

1. **IOS is the strongest segment economically** on both Q2 2025 and FY2024 measures. Its Q2 2025 margin of **24.67%** is 687 bps above Precision/Ralliant and 1,243 bps above AHS; its FY2024 margin of **25.95%** was also the highest among the three segments.
2. **Precision/Ralliant was historically high-margin but deteriorated sharply**. FY2024 margin was **22.43%**, close to IOS, but Q2 2025 fell to **17.80%**, the largest deterioration among the segments.
3. **AHS remains the lowest-margin segment**, at **12.24%** in Q2 2025, but it was the only segment with a positive Q2 2025 versus FY2024 margin trajectory.

This means Fortive’s remaining business is not simply a “higher-margin” portfolio in every historical period. On a FY2024 combined segment basis, the three segments together generated a **21.83%** margin, while retained IOS+AHS generated **21.49%**; removing Precision at its FY2024 **22.43%** margin was slightly margin-dilutive. On the Q2 2025 run-rate, however, the three segments together generated **19.69%**, while retained IOS+AHS generated **20.68%**; removing Precision at its Q2 2025 **17.80%** margin was margin-accretive. That shift is central to the strategic interpretation.

## Revenue momentum: 2023 to 2024

| Segment | FY2023 sales | FY2024 sales | 2023-to-2024 revenue growth |
|---|---:|---:|---:|
| Intelligent Operating Solutions | $2,612.2M | $2,714.7M | **+3.92%** |
| Precision Technologies / Ralliant | $2,223.7M | $2,229.4M | **+0.26%** |
| Advanced Healthcare Solutions | $1,229.4M | $1,287.7M | **+4.74%** |

Source: Fortive FY2024 annual segment sales table [4]. Growth rates are calculated as (FY2024 sales − FY2023 sales) ÷ FY2023 sales.

This revenue pattern clarifies the portfolio trade-off:

- **IOS = high-margin / moderate-growth core.** IOS combines the highest margins with respectable FY2024 growth of **+3.92%**. It is the retained portfolio’s economic anchor.
- **AHS = faster-growth / lower-margin improvement platform.** AHS grew the fastest at **+4.74%** and was the only segment whose Q2 2025 margin improved versus FY2024, but its absolute margin of **12.24%** remains well below IOS and Precision/Ralliant.
- **Precision/Ralliant = historically high-margin / low-growth / deteriorating near-term profile.** PT’s FY2024 growth was almost flat at **+0.26%**, and its margin fell from **22.43%** in FY2024 to **17.80%** in Q2 2025. That does not make the business structurally unattractive, but it does support Fortive’s case that PT had different investment needs and a different cycle than the retained workflow/healthcare portfolio.

On revenue momentum alone, the separation leaves Fortive with a faster-growing 2024 base: retained IOS+AHS grew **+4.19%** from 2023 to 2024, compared with **+2.75%** for the pre-spin three-segment portfolio including flat Precision.

This decline matters because it limits the strength of any near-term claim that the separation had already improved Fortive’s financial performance. Before Ralliant was treated as discontinued operations, consolidated sales were lower and operating margin contracted materially year over year. In other words, the portfolio action is best read as a strategic repositioning and capital-allocation reset rather than evidence of an already-realized margin inflection.

## Segment business mix and strategic role

Fortive’s retained and separated businesses have different operating exposures:

- **Intelligent Operating Solutions (retained):** IOS provides advanced instrumentation, software, and services for mission-critical workflows, including electrical test and measurement, facility and asset lifecycle software, and connected-worker safety and compliance across manufacturing, process industries, healthcare, utilities/power, communications, and electronics end markets [4]. Strategically, IOS is the high-margin retained core, combining workflow software/services and sensing/safety exposure with the best unit economics.
- **Advanced Healthcare Solutions (retained):** AHS supplies healthcare workflow and instrumentation solutions, including instrument sterilization, instrument tracking, biomedical test tools, radiation detection and safety monitoring, and clinical productivity software and solutions [4]. Strategically, AHS gives Fortive healthcare workflow exposure and the fastest 2023-to-2024 segment growth, but it is the lower-margin platform that must continue improving mix and operating leverage.
- **Precision Technologies / Ralliant (separated):** PT/Ralliant covers test, measurement, sensing, and precision technologies, including electrical test and measurement, sensing/material technologies, grid monitoring/protection, energetic materials, and precision safety systems across industrial, power/energy, automotive, medical equipment, food and beverage, aerospace/defense, semiconductor, communications, and electronics markets [7] [6]. Ralliant’s businesses include Test and Measurement brands such as Tektronix, Keithley Instruments, Sonix, and EA Elektro-Automatik, and Sensors and Safety Systems brands including Qualitrol, Gems Sensors, Setra Systems, Hengstler Dynapar, Anderson-Negele, Dover Motion, Specialty Product Technologies, and Pacific Scientific Energetic Materials [6].

The post-spin Fortive portfolio is therefore more concentrated in operating workflows, safety/productivity, software/services, and healthcare operations, while Ralliant becomes the dedicated precision technology and measurement company.

## Management rationale and capital allocation implications

Fortive and Ralliant framed the separation as a way to create **two independent public companies** with clearer strategic mandates. The stated benefits included enhanced management focus, distinct boards and management teams, improved operational agility, tailored capital allocation, separate optimal capital structures, independent equity structures, selective M&A aligned with each company’s core strategy, and avoiding internal competition for investment capital [7] [8]. Fortive described the remaining company as focused on IOS and AHS, with resilient, high-quality recurring growth, approximately **50% recurring revenue**, productivity and safety solutions for industrial and healthcare operations, organic compounding, and balanced capital allocation; Ralliant would focus on Precision Technologies and customer-centric innovation in secular growth industries [7] [8].

The capital allocation mechanics were material. Before the distribution, Ralliant borrowed **$1.15 billion** and used the proceeds to fund a **$1.15 billion cash dividend** to Fortive as partial consideration for the transfer of PT assets and liabilities [3] [7]. Fortive disclosed that it intended to use Ralliant dividend proceeds to repay debt — including about **$302 million** for part of its 2026 euro-denominated notes, **$324 million** for its Euro term loan, and **$98 million** for its Yen term loan — and to repurchase shares through a special purpose share repurchase program allowing up to **$550 million** of Fortive common stock purchases exclusively from Ralliant cash proceeds [3].

That capital allocation design reinforces the strategic logic: Fortive used the separation not only to narrow its industrial/healthcare workflow portfolio, but also to reset leverage and shareholder-return capacity. Ralliant, conversely, begins as a focused precision-technology company with its own capital structure, equity currency, and investment priorities.

## Assessment: has the separation strengthened Fortive’s competitive positioning?

The evidence supports a **qualified yes strategically, but mixed financially in the near term**.

**What strengthened:**

- Fortive now owns the segment with the best unit economics, IOS, and no longer allocates management attention and capital between IOS/AHS and a precision-technology platform with different end markets and cycles.
- The retained IOS+AHS portfolio had better 2023-to-2024 revenue momentum than the full three-segment portfolio: **+4.19%** for retained IOS+AHS versus **+2.75%** including Precision.
- On Q2 2025 segment economics, removing Precision was margin-accretive to the retained mix: retained IOS+AHS margin was **20.68%** versus **19.69%** for all three segments, because Precision’s Q2 margin had fallen to **17.80%**.
- AHS, while low-margin, showed the best margin trajectory, with Q2 2025 margin up **+15 bps** versus FY2024, suggesting Fortive retains a platform with room for operating leverage.
- The separation directly aligns with management’s stated focus on clearer capital allocation, recurring workflow exposure, and separate investment priorities for Fortive and Ralliant [7] [8].

**What weakens or complicates the case:**

- On a FY2024 basis, Precision was still a high-profit contributor at **22.43%** segment margin. Removing it from the FY2024 mix would have reduced combined segment margin from **21.83%** to **21.49%**.
- IOS margins softened from **25.95%** in FY2024 to **24.67%** in Q2 2025, and AHS remains much lower-margin at **12.24%**.
- Fortive’s total-company GAAP operating margin fell **398 bps** year over year in Q1 2025, so consolidated margin evidence before discontinued-operations treatment does not yet show that the portfolio has become financially stronger [5].
- Ralliant’s own standalone Q2 2025 results show post-spin cost and demand pressures: sales **$503.3 million**, operating profit **$58.9 million**, and operating margin **11.7%**, down from **19.7%** in Q2 2024 on that standalone basis [6]. That supports the separation logic but also shows that spin-related and cyclical pressures are real.

Overall, Fortive appears to be prioritizing **focus, recurring workflow exposure, and capital discipline over retaining every high-margin earnings stream**. IOS is the clear economic core; AHS is the growth-and-improvement platform; Precision/Ralliant was a valuable but slower-growing and recently margin-compressing business better suited to its own capital allocation and market narrative. The separation has likely strengthened the **strategic coherence** of Fortive’s remaining business units, but the financial verdict remains conditional: it will be confirmed only if IOS maintains high margins, AHS converts faster growth into sustained operating leverage, and the loss of Precision’s historical profit contribution is offset by better organic growth, capital deployment, and post-spin cost discipline.
