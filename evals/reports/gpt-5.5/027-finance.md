# Array Technologies segment portfolio margin analysis (Q1 2023–Q3/9M 2024)

**Bottom line:** Array Technologies’ segment data show a small and inconsistent Array Legacy Operations gross-margin advantage during 2023, followed by a large and persistent 2024 premium. On a comparable, revised basis, the Array Legacy minus STI gross-margin gap moved from **+1.4 percentage points in Q1 2023**, **+3.1 pp in Q2 2023**, **-3.8 pp in Q3 2023** and **+15.7 pp in derived Q4 2023**, to **+27.5 pp in Q1 2024**, **+29.5 pp in Q2 2024** and **+23.3 pp in Q3 2024**. For the first nine months, the gap widened from **+0.3 pp in 9M 2023** to **+26.7 pp in 9M 2024**. That pattern makes Array Legacy the portfolio’s higher-margin profit engine in 2024, while STI remains strategically valuable for international tracker exposure but margin-dilutive unless integration, pricing, procurement, project-selection and product-standardization priorities close the gap.

## Segment definitions and measurement basis

Array’s segment framework is built around two operating/reportable segments: **Array Legacy Operations** and **STI Operations**. Array’s segment footnote states that the company operates in the U.S. and internationally, primarily in **Spain and Brazil**, and is expanding into other international markets through STI Operations; **Array Legacy Operations** consists primarily of amounts earned from the design and delivery, or later-described design, manufacture and sale, of utility-scale solar tracker systems/solar arrays in the **United States**, while **STI Operations** consists primarily of comparable amounts earned outside the **United States** [1] [2]. The CODM is the chief executive officer, and the CODM assesses each operating segment using **gross profit**, which Array says is used in the annual budget and forecasting process and monthly segment reviews [1]. In the later expanded segment tables, gross profit is explicitly reconciled from **segment revenue less product cost, amortization of developed technology, depreciation and other costs**, so the gross-margin calculations below use **gross profit divided by segment revenue** [1].

## Revenue and gross-profit contribution mix

| Period | Array Legacy revenue share | STI revenue share | Array Legacy gross-profit share | STI gross-profit share | Interpretation |
|---|---:|---:|---:|---:|---|
| FY 2023 | 74.4% | 25.6% | 76.4% | 23.6% | STI was meaningful scale and only mildly dilutive because its 24.3% margin was close to Array Legacy’s 27.1% [7]. |
| 9M 2023 | 72.5% | 27.5% | 72.7% | 27.3% | The segments contributed gross profit almost in line with revenue, reflecting near-margin parity [6]. |
| 9M 2024 | 71.8% | 28.2% | 87.6% | 12.4% | STI still supplied more than one-quarter of segment revenue but only one-eighth of segment gross profit, showing a material 2024 drag on consolidated gross margin [6]. |

The mix shift is central to portfolio interpretation. In FY 2023, Array Legacy generated **74.4%** of combined segment revenue and **76.4%** of segment gross profit; STI generated **25.6%** of revenue and **23.6%** of gross profit, so STI added geographic reach without a large gross-margin penalty [7]. By 9M 2024, the revenue mix was similar—Array Legacy **71.8%**, STI **28.2%**—but gross-profit mix diverged sharply: Array Legacy produced **87.6%** of segment gross profit and STI only **12.4%** [6].

## Evolution of the margin gap

The time series shows three distinct phases:

1. **Near parity in early 2023.** Array Legacy’s premium was small in Q1 2023 (**+1.4 pp**) and Q2 2023 (**+3.1 pp**) [3] [4]. For 9M 2023, the segments were essentially tied: Array Legacy **26.9%** versus STI **26.6%**, a **+0.3 pp** gap [6].
2. **Temporary inversion in Q3 2023.** STI outperformed Array Legacy in Q3 2023, with STI at **27.6%** versus Array Legacy at **23.8%**, a **-3.8 pp** Legacy-minus-STI gap [6]. This inversion means 2023 did not yet show a structurally inferior STI margin profile; STI could match or exceed Array Legacy in at least one quarter.
3. **Sharp and persistent 2024 separation.** The gap widened abruptly in Q4 2023 to **+15.7 pp** on a derived basis, then persisted above **+23 pp** in every quarter of 2024 through September: **+27.5 pp** in Q1, **+29.5 pp** in Q2 and **+23.3 pp** in Q3 [3] [4] [6] [7]. On the comparable year-to-date basis, the gap expanded from **+0.3 pp in 9M 2023** to **+26.7 pp in 9M 2024** [6].

The 2024 pattern is not merely a revenue-cycle artifact: STI’s revenue share stayed material at **28.2%** of 9M 2024 combined segment revenue, but its gross-profit share collapsed to **12.4%** because its 9M gross margin was only **15.1%**, versus Array Legacy’s **41.8%** [6].

## Operational efficiency versus strategic value

**Operational efficiency.** Array Legacy is the clearly more efficient gross-profit engine in 2024. Its gross margin rose from **26.9% in 9M 2023** to **41.8% in 9M 2024**, while STI fell from **26.6%** to **15.1%** on the same year-to-date basis [6]. Array’s 2023 discussion attributed the improvement in Array Legacy gross profit as a percentage of revenue to improved pass-through of commodity volatility to customers, cost savings in logistics and raw materials, and a higher proportion of higher-margin non-tracker revenue; it attributed STI’s 2023 margin improvement to improved commodity pass-through and raw-material cost savings [10]. The later 2024 result implies those gross-margin benefits were more durable or more powerful in Array Legacy than in STI.

**Strategic value.** STI still has strategic value because it gives Array international reach that the legacy U.S. business does not provide on its own. Array’s acquisition rationale for STI Norland was to establish Array as a global tracker leader with positions in North America, Latin America and Europe; create the largest solar tracker company in the world with manufacturing capacity and design/engineering resources on three continents; establish Array in the Brazilian market; and provide immediate presence in Spain, Western Europe, Brazil and South Africa [11] [10]. STI’s operations also align with Array’s international footprint: STI designs, manufactures and sells utility-scale solar tracker systems in global markets including Spain, Brazil, South Africa and Australia [10].

The trade-off is that the 2024 margin evidence separates **strategic reach** from **economic efficiency**. STI is not valueless: it supplied **$180.768 million** of 9M 2024 revenue, or **28.2%** of combined segment revenue [6]. But because it produced only **$27.240 million** of 9M 2024 gross profit, or **12.4%** of combined segment gross profit, it was materially margin-dilutive to the overall portfolio at the gross-profit level [6]. Array Legacy, by contrast, supplied **$459.807 million** of 9M 2024 revenue but **$192.118 million** of gross profit, demonstrating that it was the principal source of gross-profit dollars and margin quality [6].

## Acquisition and integration implications

Array acquired **100% of Soluciones Técnicas Integrales Norland, S.L.U.**, a Spanish private limited liability company and its subsidiaries, on **January 11, 2022** [12] [10]. The purchase consideration was **$410.5 million in cash plus 13,894,800 Array common shares**, with total fair value of consideration of **$610.8 million** [10]. The detailed annual filing table shows **$409.647 million** of cash consideration for STI, **$0.896 million** of cash consideration for STI transaction expenses, **$410.543 million** total cash consideration, **$200.224 million** of non-cash equity consideration and **$610.767 million** total consideration transferred [10].

The 2023 margin evidence initially supported the deal’s integration thesis: STI reached **26.6%** gross margin in 9M 2023, nearly equal to Array Legacy’s **26.9%**, and exceeded Array Legacy in Q3 2023 by **3.8 pp** [6]. That convergence was consistent with the acquisition rationale—international expansion, complementary tracker products, diversified supply chain, and supplier/fixed-cost scale opportunities [11] [13].

The sustained 2024 gap changes the priority. With Array Legacy at **41.8%** gross margin and STI at **15.1%** for 9M 2024, the acquisition still provides market access but has not delivered sustained margin convergence through September 2024 [6]. Integration should therefore be evaluated less by revenue expansion alone and more by whether STI can close a roughly **26.7 pp** year-to-date gross-margin deficit. The operational priorities implied by the data are:

- **Pricing discipline:** move STI contracts closer to Array Legacy’s ability to preserve gross margin through commodity and input-cost pass-through, because Array’s 2023 discussion identified improved commodity pass-through as a gross-margin driver [10].
- **Procurement and supply-chain efficiency:** apply supplier-scale benefits and raw-material cost savings across STI, because Array cited raw-material savings as a margin driver and the acquisition rationale included scale benefits with suppliers [10] [13].
- **Project selection:** favor STI geographies and customer/project types that can sustain margins closer to Array Legacy’s 2024 level; low-margin international growth is dilutive when STI contributes **28.2%** of 9M 2024 revenue but only **12.4%** of gross profit [6].
- **Product standardization and complementary technology transfer:** use Array/STI product complementarity to reduce customization and execution variability, consistent with Array’s statement that STI brought complementary products and technology to the global tracker market [11].
- **Overhead and fixed-cost absorption:** use STI’s international revenue base to absorb fixed manufacturing, design and engineering resources, because the acquisition was justified partly by manufacturing capacity and design/engineering resources on three continents and fixed-cost leverage [11] [13].

If STI’s margins converge again—as they did in 9M 2023 and Q3 2023—that would support the acquisition case by showing that global diversification and cross-selling can be achieved without persistent gross-margin dilution. If the 2024 gap persists, Array’s portfolio optimization logic points to limiting incremental investment in persistently low-return STI markets or reallocating capital and management attention toward higher-return Array Legacy opportunities, while retaining STI selectively where it delivers strategic access or project economics that approach the group’s gross-margin threshold.

## Later filings and comparability through June 23, 2026

Later filings available through June 23, 2026 continued to present **Array Legacy Operations** and **STI Operations** as the two reportable segments rather than discontinuing them. The 2025 Form 10-K states that Array reports results in two segments—Array Legacy Operations and STI Operations—and also states that the **APA** acquisition is reported within the **Array Legacy Operations** segment, not as a replacement or discontinuation of STI [14]. The Q1 2026 Form 10-Q likewise states that Array has two reportable segments, **Array Legacy Operations** and **STI Operations**, with Array Legacy covering solar arrays in the U.S. and STI covering solar arrays outside the U.S.; it also says APA is a component of Array Legacy Operations [15].

The principal comparability adjustment for the periods analyzed is the use of later comparative values for Q1 and Q2 2023 gross profit: the Q1 2024 10-Q comparative table reports Q1 2023 Array Legacy gross profit of **$79.835 million** rather than the earlier Q1 2023 filing’s **$83.474 million** [3] [8], and the Q2 2024 10-Q comparative table reports Q2 2023 Array Legacy gross profit of **$102.950 million** rather than the earlier Q2 2023 filing’s **$106.590 million** [4] [9]. The 2024 and later expanded segment tables did not rename, reclassify or discontinue the segment names for the Q1 2023 through 9M 2024 analysis; they expanded the segment reconciliation to show cost components and continued to use gross profit as the CODM’s segment performance measure [1] [2] [15] [14].
