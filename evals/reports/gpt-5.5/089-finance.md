**Bottom line:** For a **2-year horizon**, DSP Global Innovation Overseas Equity Omni FoF is the better fit on the evidence available: it is globally diversified across several innovation/technology vehicles and had stronger end-2024 NAV momentum — 20.80% 1-year return and 14.02% since-inception CAGR to 31 Dec 2024 — while Edelweiss Greater China returned 14.76% over one year but -6.69% CAGR over three years to 30 Dec 2024 [1][2]. ## 1) Portfolio composition and concentration

### Edelweiss Greater China Equity Offshore Fund

Edelweiss is best read as a **single-region overseas feeder**. The stated objective is to invest in JPMorgan Funds – JF Greater China Equity Fund, which invests primarily in companies incorporated in, registered in, or deriving the predominant part of their economic activity from the Greater China region [3]. The benchmark shown on the ET factsheet page is **MSCI Golden Dragon Index** [3]. That means the fund’s main portfolio risk is not stock-count concentration at the Indian FoF level but **regional, currency, regulatory and geopolitical concentration** in Greater China.

The 31 Dec 2024 portfolio statement shows the Indian fund’s top-level portfolio was **JPM Greater China-I-I2 USD 60.80%**, **JPM Greater China-I AC 38.09%**, **TREPS/reverse repo 1.67%**, and **net receivables/payables -0.56%**, with **98.89%** in the two JPM Greater China fund-unit lines [5]. Therefore, top-level holding concentration is high, while the stock diversification is inside the JPMorgan underlying fund.

## Recommendation by horizon

### 2-year horizon: choose DSP Global Innovation Overseas Omni FoF

For a **2-year horizon**, DSP is the stronger choice on the metrics available. Its NAV rose **20.80%** over the one-year period to 31 Dec 2024 and compounded at **14.02%** from 15 Feb 2022 to 31 Dec 2024, compared with Edelweiss’s **14.76%** one-year return and **-6.69%** three-year CAGR to 30 Dec 2024 [1][2]. Its portfolio spreads risk across several overseas funds/ETFs rather than one regional feeder, and by construction it is exposed to global innovation, Nasdaq 100, medical technology and precision medicine themes, with only 9.74% in China Internet ETF at the disclosed top level [6]. The trade-off is that the allocation is still highly theme-concentrated and the current TER evidence is higher than Edelweiss, at 1.85%–2.03% versus Edelweiss’s 1.57% [10][9][3].

For a 2-year investor, that trade-off is acceptable relative to Edelweiss because Edelweiss is a narrower Greater China macro/regulatory bet; if China sentiment or policy disappoints, the portfolio has few non-China offsets. DSP is still aggressive, but its geographic and vehicle diversification makes it the more suitable 2-year option.

### 5-year horizon: choose Edelweiss Greater China only as a China satellite; otherwise neither is a core 5-year global allocation

For a **5-year horizon**, Edelweiss is the more usable comparison candidate because it has a full **5-year Dec-2024 NAV record** — **3.17% CAGR** from NAV 32.610 on 30 Dec 2019 to NAV 38.112 on 30 Dec 2024 — whereas DSP did not have five years of live performance by that date [2][1][10]. Edelweiss also has the lower stated expense ratio at 1.57% [3].

The recommendation is therefore **Edelweiss for a 5-year horizon only if the intended allocation is a dedicated Greater China satellite**. It is not the better diversified global fund; it is the fund with the longer record, lower public TER, and cleaner regional mandate. If the investor wants a broad 5-year global equity allocation, neither fund is ideal: Edelweiss is too geographically concentrated, and DSP’s Dec-2024 5-year record does not exist.
